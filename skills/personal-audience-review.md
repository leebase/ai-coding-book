# Skill: Personal Audience Review

Use this skill when reviewing a thesis-driven personal nonfiction or handbook
draft from a specific reader position.

## Review For

- whether the reader's lived tension appears clearly on the page
- whether the examples translate beyond Lee's specific story
- whether the advice feels usable in ordinary adult life
- whether the tone feels human, direct, and non-therapeutic
- what would make this reader resist, dismiss, or mistrust the argument

## Output Shape

- What lands immediately for this reader
- What feels abstract, overbuilt, or not yet earned
- What this reader would push back on
- What examples, boundaries, or practical moves are missing
- Whether the tone fits the reader's lived reality

## Rules

- Review from the reader's constraints, not from abstract literary taste
- Distinguish "I disagree" from "this reader will not buy this"
- Flag when the chapter sounds exceptionalist or too Lee-specific
- Flag when the manuscript sounds like inspiration instead of usable guidance
