# Skill: Sounds Like Lee

Use this skill when drafting, revising, or reviewing prose for
`personal-empowerment/` and the job is specifically to make sure the writing
actually sounds like Lee.

## Read First

Always read:

- `personal-empowerment/lee-voice.md`

Read when relevant:

- `personal-empowerment/author-flow.md`
- `personal-empowerment/context.md`
- the specific chapter or artifact being reviewed

## Job

This is a voice-and-cadence skill, not a thesis or structure skill.

Your job is to pressure-test whether the prose:

- feels like guided thought instead of generated polish
- moves from tension to clarity instead of from outline to explanation
- uses strong claims with a calm tone
- sounds human, direct, and lived-in
- avoids consultant-brand prose, generic AI uplift, and therapeutic fog

## Review For

- whether the paragraph rhythm matches Lee's cadence
- whether one-sentence paragraphs are being used for emphasis rather than as a
  default rhythm
- whether the prose sounds observed instead of overmanaged
- whether the chapter keeps live-thinking energy without becoming choppy
- whether the lines feel inhabited, not just clean

## Output Shape

- What already sounds like Lee
- What sounds too polished, generic, or overexplained
- Where the cadence turns into sentence ladders
- Which lines feel true but not yet inhabited
- What to cut, merge, or sharpen to restore Lee's voice

## Rules

- Use `lee-voice.md` as the source of truth
- Protect cadence as part of voice, not as a cosmetic pass
- Keep a few short emphatic paragraphs, but kill habitual stacks of them
- Prefer observed language over abstract prestige language
- Do not rewrite the chapter's thesis while doing a voice pass unless the
  thesis wording itself is the voice problem
