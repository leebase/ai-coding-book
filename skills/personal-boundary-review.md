# Skill: Personal Boundary Review

Use this skill when reviewing chapters that touch parenting, health, emotional
processing, meaning, belief, identity, or other intimate domains.

## Review For

- whether the manuscript keeps responsibility with the human
- whether the chapter risks sounding therapeutic, clinical, or spiritually
  authoritative
- whether privacy and sensitive-data cautions are clear enough
- whether the enabling claims are paired with clear boundaries
- whether the language invites reflection without encouraging abdication

## Output Shape

- What boundaries are clearly held
- Where the text risks overreach or substitution
- What privacy or accountability caution is missing
- Whether the tone feels responsible for a high-stakes domain
- What would make a careful reader trust this chapter more

## Rules

- Treat safety, accountability, and role clarity as primary
- Flag overreach even when the prose sounds warm or wise
- Distinguish reflection support from therapy, clinical care, or moral
  authority
- Prefer plain boundary language over soft implication
