## Core Claim

This book is not primarily about productivity.

It is about how AI can expand a human being:

- agency
- judgment
- capability
- expression
- identity

AI should function as a thinking partner and amplifier, not as a replacement
for responsibility, authorship, or meaning.

This book also sits in the same thesis family as the consulting-growth and
business-growth books:

- use AI to amplify humans
- create abundance
- reject efficiency-only logic when it becomes a replacement ideology

Do not let the book drift toward "here are 50 prompts to improve your life."
The value is not a bag of prompts. The value is a way of becoming more capable.

## Non-Negotiable Terms

### RBM

`RBM` means `Real Boy Moment`.

It is an AI-side event:

- the AI says something stunning
- it feels emergent rather than formulaic
- it hits hard enough to trigger a prior-art check

Do not use `RBM` to describe Lee's own insight.

### MoG

`MoG` means `Moment of Genius`.

It is the human-side counterpart:

- Lee sees, names, forges, or synthesizes something real
- the leap belongs to the human, not the model

Do not flatten `RBM` and `MoG` into synonyms.

## Writing Rules

- Use the live-thinking, tension-to-clarity voice in `lee-voice.md`
- Keep empowerment concrete, not vague
- Name patterns only when they create real leverage
- Stories exist to prove a principle, not to build persona
- Do not drift into generic AI boosterism
- Do not drift into consultant-brand prose
- Do not confuse fast output with real transformation
- Do not turn chapters into prompt dumps or hack lists

## When an RBM Happens

If an AI output feels like an RBM:

1. capture the exact wording or concept
2. pause instead of instantly celebrating it
3. check whether the thought already exists in the wild
4. classify it as new, rediscovered, or newly framed
5. decide what the human contribution is from there

The book should treat emergence seriously without becoming naive about novelty.
