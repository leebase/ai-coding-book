# Skill: Research Synthesis

Use this skill when turning source material into support for a nonfiction
argument.

## For Each Theme

- Summarize what the best sources actually say
- Note whether the support is empirical, analytical, or opinion
- Record where credible sources disagree
- Identify where the book is inferring beyond the sources
- Flag what still needs firsthand reporting or stronger evidence

## Output Shape

- Claim
- Evidence that supports it
- Evidence that complicates it
- Confidence: strong / moderate / weak
- Research gap, if any

## Rules

- Do not let one prestigious source stand in for consensus.
- Separate citation-worthy material from useful-but-speculative ideas.
- Prefer primary sources and named thinkers over summary sludge.
- Treat a vivid statistic as a lead, not truth, until the exact source is verified.
