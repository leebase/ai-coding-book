# Service Portfolio Triage

> Playbook artifact for sorting consulting offers in the post-delivery economy.

---

## Core Question

Which parts of the current service portfolio are still defendable, which need
to be redesigned, which should be exited, and which need to move up the value
stack before repricing catches them?

---

## Four Portfolio Buckets

1. Defend
2. Redesign
3. Sunset
4. Climb Above

Use the buckets for current offers, not imagined future ones.

---

## Quick Definitions

### Defend

Keep and strengthen this offer if it is still differentiated, still hard to
replace with cheaper delivery, and still connected to higher-value trust,
judgment, or outcome ownership.

### Redesign

Keep the underlying client need, but change the offer shape, scope, pricing, or
deliverables so the firm is no longer selling visible effort as the main value.

### Sunset

Exit the offer over time if it is increasingly commodity, easy to compare on
price, hard to defend as premium, and no longer strategic to the firm.

### Climb Above

Move the conversation upstream if the current offer is still useful but too
close to delivery labor. Reposition around diagnosis, redesign, enablement,
governance, capability building, or higher-order problem ownership.

---

## Triage Questions

Ask these for each service line or offer:

1. Is the client primarily buying judgment or labor?
2. Has AI reduced the visible effort in a way the client can now notice?
3. Is the offer easy to compare against cheaper alternatives?
4. Does the work create trust that leads to higher-value follow-on work?
5. Is the team being paid for outcomes, direction, or mostly for staffed effort?
6. If this offer shrank by 30 percent, would the firm still want to build its
   future around it?
7. What higher-value work could this offer lead into if repositioned well?

---

## Warning Signs of the Wrong Work

Take a hard look when an offer shows several of these signs:

- pricing is still tied mostly to hours or headcount
- clients ask why the work should still cost this much
- AI mainly makes the offer faster rather than more valuable
- the team keeps defending effort instead of outcomes
- the work produces little reusable knowledge, method, or relationship depth
- the offer is busy but no longer strategic

---

## Reader Outcome

After using this tool, a consulting leader should be able to:

- sort current offers into defend, redesign, sunset, or climb above
- identify which revenue lines are most exposed to delivery repricing
- choose where the firm should stop relying on visible effort as the business
