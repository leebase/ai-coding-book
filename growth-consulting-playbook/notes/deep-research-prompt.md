# Deep Research Prompt

Use the prompt below with a research-capable AI model.

---

You are conducting deep research for a nonfiction business book with the working title:

**The Growth Consulting Playbook: How Firms Grow When Software Delivery No Longer Pays the Bills**

## Research Goal

Find and synthesize the best existing thought leadership, research, commentary, and evidence related to this thesis:

- AI is driving down the marginal cost of software delivery and code production.
- Consulting firms that primarily monetize coding labor, implementation labor, or software delivery hours may face margin compression and commoditization.
- Many firms and clients will instinctively use AI productivity gains to reduce headcount.
- That strategy can destroy institutional knowledge, continuity, and long-term growth capacity.
- The stronger long-term strategy is to use AI to empower employees, redesign work, and convert productivity gains into growth.
- AI may redefine which employees are most strategically valuable to retain.
- Consulting firms that help clients do this well may become more valuable, not less.

## Core Questions To Answer

Answer these questions with evidence and citations:

1. What respected thinkers, firms, analysts, academics, or executives have already published ideas adjacent to this thesis?
2. What has been written about AI reducing the cost of software development, coding labor, or implementation work?
3. What has been written about AI-driven productivity gains being used for headcount reduction?
4. What evidence exists that layoffs or over-optimization for efficiency can destroy tacit knowledge, continuity, or organizational learning?
5. What has been written about using AI to augment employees instead of replace them?
6. What thought leadership exists on “growth from productivity” versus “cost savings from productivity”?
7. What have consulting firms themselves published about the future of consulting in the AI era?
8. What have major enterprise buyers, analysts, or industry researchers said about how consulting business models may change as AI commoditizes parts of delivery?
9. What arguments contradict this thesis, and what evidence supports those counterarguments?
10. Where are the strongest gaps in the existing literature that this book could uniquely fill?

## Source Quality Requirements

Prioritize sources in this order:

1. Primary sources:
   - Consulting firm reports
   - Annual letters or executive essays
   - Analyst reports
   - Academic research
   - Conference talks, keynote transcripts, or official interviews
   - Original essays or articles by named thought leaders
2. High-quality secondary sources:
   - Major business publications
   - Reputable tech and management publications
   - Well-reported journalism summarizing primary evidence
3. Use lower-quality sources only if they surface an important idea that is not available elsewhere, and label them clearly.

Do not rely on generic AI-generated summaries, SEO content, or unattributed hot takes.

Every substantial claim in the output must be traceable to a citable source.

## Research Scope

Include material from:

- Management thinkers
- Strategy scholars
- Organizational behavior researchers
- Labor economists
- Enterprise AI adoption researchers
- Consulting industry analysts
- Major consulting firms
- Software industry leaders

Specifically look for research and commentary connected to:

- AI and productivity
- software engineering commoditization
- consulting business model shifts
- labor substitution vs augmentation
- knowledge loss from layoffs
- tacit knowledge and organizational memory
- workforce redesign
- employee empowerment with AI
- growth strategy enabled by automation
- the future of professional services

## Deliverable Requirements

Produce a single Markdown document.

The document must be self-contained and written for an author preparing a serious nonfiction book proposal.

Use this exact structure:

# Research Memo: Thought Leadership on AI, Consulting, and Growth

## 1. Executive Summary
- Summarize the most important findings in 8-12 bullets.
- State whether the thesis appears well supported, partially supported, novel, or weakly supported.

## 2. Core Thesis Framing
- Restate the proposed book thesis in cleaner language.
- Identify the strongest adjacent ideas already in the market.
- Identify what seems original about this angle.

## 3. Key Thinkers and Institutions
For each thinker, firm, or institution:
- Name
- Why they matter
- Their relevant argument
- Whether they support, complicate, or contradict the thesis
- 1-3 citable sources with links

## 4. Evidence by Theme
Use subsections for each:
- AI reduces the cost of software delivery
- AI and headcount reduction
- augmentation vs replacement
- knowledge loss, continuity, and tacit knowledge
- productivity redirected into growth
- future of consulting and professional services
- changing definition of valuable employees

For each subsection:
- Summarize the evidence
- Include direct source-backed takeaways
- Link every source

## 5. Counterarguments and Tensions
- List the strongest opposing views
- Include who argues them
- Explain what evidence supports them
- Explain where the thesis may need nuance

## 6. Implications for Book Positioning
- What this book can say that existing thought leadership does not say clearly
- Which claims feel crowded
- Which claims feel fresh
- Recommended positioning angles
- Recommended audiences

## 7. Source Table
Create a table with these columns:
- Theme
- Source
- Author / Organization
- Date
- Type
- Key claim
- URL
- Usefulness to the book (High / Medium / Low)

## 8. Annotated Bibliography
For the 15-25 most useful sources:
- Full citation in a consistent format
- 2-4 sentence annotation
- Note how the source could be used in the book

## 9. Research Gaps
- What still needs firsthand reporting, interviews, or proprietary evidence
- Which claims are currently more conceptual than proven
- Which future chapters would need stronger evidence

## 10. Appendix: Quotable Lines
- Include short quotable excerpts only where allowed
- Keep quotes short and attributable
- Include source links

## Citation Rules

- Include inline links throughout.
- Include publication dates.
- Distinguish clearly between direct evidence and inference.
- Do not invent agreement where sources only partially overlap with the thesis.
- If sources disagree, show the disagreement.
- If a source is opinion rather than empirical research, label it as opinion or commentary.

## Research Method Rules

- Search broadly first, then narrow.
- Prefer the most recent credible sources when discussing AI capabilities or consulting market changes.
- Also include older foundational sources when discussing tacit knowledge, organizational continuity, layoffs, or professional services economics.
- Avoid duplication and repetitive summaries.
- Do not pad the output.
- If evidence is thin on any part of the thesis, say so explicitly.

## Output Standard

The final Markdown should be publication-grade research support for an author.
It should be dense, specific, skeptical, and useful.
It should help the author decide:

1. whether this book thesis is genuinely differentiated
2. which claims are strongest
3. which claims need more evidence
4. which sources should seed the project's permanent context
