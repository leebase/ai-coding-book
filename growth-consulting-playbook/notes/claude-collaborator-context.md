# Claude Collaborator Context

> Notes and outline framing contributed by Claude during early concept development. Treat as input for comparison and synthesis, not as a locked direction.

## High-Level Framing

- Optional foreword premise: the most dangerous consulting engagement may be the one where a firm helps a client cut its way to the future.
- Central idea: build the book around a **Multiplier Model**.
- Structural emphasis: make the case, force the decision point, then provide a concrete playbook.

## Proposed Structure

### Act I — The Trap

Goal: Name the dynamic and give readers language for what they are already feeling.

1. **The Delivery Economy Is Ending**
   - AI compresses the cost of software delivery, automation, and workflow implementation.
   - Consulting firms built on selling human delivery hours face structural pressure.

2. **The First Trap: The Easy Win**
   - Headcount reduction is the most visible AI win.
   - Efficiency without a growth thesis removes knowledge, continuity, judgment, and future capacity.
   - Named concept: **The Knowledge Drain**

3. **The Second Trap: The Consultant's Complicity**
   - Consulting firms often lead clients into labor substitution because it is familiar and easy to scope.
   - This can also shrink the firm's own future engagement surface.
   - Named concept: **Eating the Seed Corn**

4. **What Gets Lost When You Cut Too Deep**
   - Institutional knowledge lives in people, relationships, judgment, and informal networks.
   - AI does not automatically preserve this.

### Act II — The Fork

Goal: Introduce the strategic decision point and show who AI replaces versus multiplies.

5. **The Multiplier Model: Two Roads from One Decision**
   - Core framework chapter
   - Substitution Road vs. Empowerment Road
   - Includes a visual model

6. **Who AI Replaces, Who It Multiplies**
   - Role taxonomy: substitute, augment, elevate
   - Highest-value employees may be those carrying context, relationships, and judgment
   - Named concept: **Knowledge Carriers**

7. **The Growth Thesis Every Engagement Needs**
   - Every AI engagement should answer: where does the productivity go?
   - Productivity not redirected into growth eventually disappears into margin pressure.

8. **What Clients Actually Need (And Can't See Yet)**
   - Clients ask for speed, cost reduction, and automation.
   - They may actually need redesigned work, retained knowledge, expanded capacity, and workforce adaptation.

### Act III — The Playbook

Goal: Make the model operational for firms of different sizes.

9. **Redesigning Your Service Portfolio**
   - Shift service lines toward workforce redesign, knowledge preservation, AI readiness, growth architecture, and human-AI workflow design

10. **The New Engagement Model**
   - Outcome-based pricing
   - Knowledge transfer as a deliverable
   - Metrics tied to client growth rather than hours delivered

11. **Talent: Who You Need Now**
   - The most valuable consultants may be those who understand organizations, broker knowledge, and connect AI capability to business context

12. **Leading the Transition Inside Your Firm**
   - Internal change management for consulting leadership
   - Practice economics, partner incentives, delivery mindset, and culture

13. **The Client Conversation That Changes Everything**
   - Reframing AI conversations from efficiency to growth
   - Includes sample language and objection handling

### Conclusion — The Multiplier Imperative

- The firms that win will not be the ones that automated the fastest.
- They will be the ones that helped clients grow the most.
- The Multiplier Model is presented as a governing philosophy, not just a service line.
