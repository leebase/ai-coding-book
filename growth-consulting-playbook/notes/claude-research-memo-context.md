# Claude Research Memo Context

> Summary takeaways from a Claude-produced research memo on thought leadership relevant to *The Growth Consulting Playbook*. This note captures the high-signal conclusions and follow-up implications. Add the full memo separately if available.

## Headline Assessment

- The book thesis is strong and genuinely differentiated.
- The individual pillars are well-supported by serious thinkers and institutions.
- The original move is reflexive: applying these frameworks back onto consulting firms themselves.
- Claimed gap: nobody has yet written the book that turns AI productivity, labor substitution, augmentation, and growth logic back onto the consulting business model in this exact way.

## Named Supporting Thinkers / Institutions

- Daron Acemoglu
- Erik Brynjolfsson
- Harvard / BCG collaboration
- McKinsey research

These are cited as evidence that major parts of the thesis are already supported, even if the combined consulting-industry framing remains open territory.

## Key Research Gaps

- The biggest gap is mid-market evidence.
- Existing published thought leadership reportedly focuses heavily on the Big Five and very large firms.
- To make the book credible for firms in the roughly $50M-$500M range, firsthand interviews with managing partners and leaders in that segment are likely needed.
- This is also flagged as an area where Lee's practical COE-building and consulting experience could provide unique value beyond academic work.

## High-Value Evidence

### McKinsey Contraction + Value Pool Framing

- A reported McKinsey finding says core tech services may face **20-30% contraction** from agentic AI.
- In the same body of thinking, McKinsey reportedly identifies **$100B-$400B in new value pools**.
- Claimed implication: this is a powerful piece of evidence for the book's central argument that firms should grow and reposition rather than simply shrink.

### METR Study

- A reported METR finding says experienced developers were **19% slower with AI while perceiving they were faster**.
- Claimed implication: this strengthens the case that implementation quality, operating model, and governed usage matter more than naive tool adoption.
- This can support a counterargument chapter and reinforce the need for higher-value consulting around adoption, workflow design, and reliable execution.

## Positioning Implications

- The research strengthens the case that the book is not generic AI commentary.
- The opportunity is to connect:
  - labor substitution
  - productivity gains
  - augmentation
  - organizational knowledge loss
  - consulting business-model transition
- The reflexive consulting-firm lens appears to be the most differentiated part of the idea.
