# Seed Blurb

**The Growth Consulting Playbook: How Firms Grow When Software Delivery No Longer Pays the Bills** is a nonfiction business book about what happens to consulting when AI drives the cost of building software and automating workflows sharply downward. For years, many consulting firms have grown by selling coding hours, implementation labor, and digital transformation projects tied to human effort. But as AI makes more of that delivery work faster, cheaper, and more abundant, the old model starts to weaken. Clients will be tempted to take the first visible win, cutting labor costs and reducing headcount. Consulting firms may even help them do it. But that logic contains a trap: when companies cut too deeply, they do not just remove cost. They also remove business knowledge, continuity, judgment, and future capacity for growth.

This book argues that the winning consulting model in the AI era will not be built primarily on efficiency or labor substitution. It will be built on helping clients use AI to empower employees, redesign work, retain critical knowledge, and redirect productivity gains into growth. AI will change which employees matter most, which capabilities firms need to protect, and what kinds of consultants remain valuable. The consultancies that thrive will be the ones that move beyond selling software delivery and instead help organizations turn AI leverage into expansion, adaptability, and new business value. The book is aimed at consulting firm leaders, practice heads, strategists, and transformation executives who need to rethink their business model before software delivery becomes too cheap to sustain it.
