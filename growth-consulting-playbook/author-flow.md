## Purpose

AuthorFlow is the adaptation of AgentFlow for thesis-driven nonfiction and business books.

This file tracks:

- how the book is being planned
- which artifacts are being created
- which collaborator patterns are proving useful
- which naming, framing, and research moves are working
- what the reusable method appears to be becoming

---

## Working Definition

AuthorFlow is a system for producing a nonfiction book by separating thinking from prose and using AI as a structured collaborator across thesis formation, story mining, research synthesis, framework design, positioning, and drafting.

The current operating idea is:

1. Start with point of view
2. Gather lived stories and raw insights
3. Triangulate with multiple AI collaborators and outside research
4. Distill named concepts and frameworks
5. Lock the argument before drafting
6. Write from the synthesis, not from the note pile

## 2026-03-27 — Product Definition Locked Before Research Completion

A v1 `product-definition.md` was drafted before all external research had arrived.

Method signal:

## 2026-03-27 — Tone Elevated to a Process Requirement

The voice requirement was made explicit: the book must sound human, inviting,
and reflective without drifting into McKinsey-style prestige prose.

Why it matters:
- The message is about human capability, not just economic repositioning.
- A bloodless consultant voice would undercut the thesis.

Method signal:
- Tone should be treated as part of method, not as a late-stage polish pass.
- AuthorFlow needs an explicit voice gate before prose is considered done.

## 2026-03-27 — Audience Review Split into Three Consulting Lenses

The review model was expanded to include three explicit audience reviewers:
- Big Consulting
- mid-market consulting
- the individual consultant

Why it matters:
- This book cannot overfit one consulting scale and still claim to be a
  practical playbook.
- Advice that feels obvious to a global firm may feel impossible to a boutique
  or solo operator.

Method signal:
- AuthorFlow should review drafts through distinct buyer/operator realities,
  not through one generic "consulting audience" lens.

## 2026-03-27 — Sprint Planning Shifted from Checklist to Roadmap

The sprint plan was rewritten from a short concept checklist into a staged
roadmap that follows the actual shape of AuthorFlow.

Why it matters:
- The work is no longer being treated as "keep gathering notes until a book appears."
- The method now recognizes distinct phases: concept synthesis, research
  integration, framework/story selection, table-of-contents design, and
  manuscript readiness.

Method signal:
- AuthorFlow benefits from explicit phase changes.
- Tactical planning for a nonfiction book should track when synthesis is still
  the right work and when drafting finally becomes justified.

## 2026-03-27 — First Canonical Concept Artifact Created

`book-concept.md` was created as the first real synthesis artifact that pulls
from raw notes, collaborator context, story inventory, and research memos.

Why it matters:
- It collapses the note pile into one working source of truth.
- It forces concept selection instead of endless accumulation.
- It makes later audience review and table-of-contents work more disciplined.

Method signal:
- AuthorFlow needs a canonical synthesis artifact before outline writing.
- This artifact should be selective: thesis, audience, concept status, story
  priority, evidence strength, and open decisions in one place.

## 2026-03-27 — Pre-Draft Package Assembled

The method moved beyond concept synthesis into a full pre-draft planning
package:

- audience review
- research support memo
- framework notes
- chapter outline
- proposal summary

Why it matters:
- This is the point where the book stops being an idea and becomes a draftable
  product.
- It also shows that AuthorFlow needs more than one synthesis artifact before
  prose begins.

Method signal:
- A strong nonfiction workflow benefits from a staged package:
  concept -> audience review -> research memo -> framework notes -> outline ->
  proposal summary -> prose

## 2026-03-27 — Third Research Memo Refined the Claim

The addition of `chatgpt-research.md` did not overturn the concept. It refined
it.

Why it matters:
- It strengthened the connection to classic consulting leverage economics.
- It sharpened the claim from "all delivery is dying" to "routine, bounded,
  repeatable delivery work is under the most pressure."
- It reinforced that the book should argue value migration, not industry death.

Method signal:
- Additional research should tighten the claim, not just bulk up the notes.

## 2026-03-27 — Human-Flourishing Material Given Structural Limits

The question of where the human-flourishing stories belong was resolved with a
clear structural rule:

- one flourishing story per act
- used as a reflective closer or pivot
- not used as a chapter anchor

Why it matters:
- It preserves the human dimension that differentiates the book.
- It prevents the manuscript from drifting into memoir or autobiographical
  sprawl.

Method signal:
- Distinctive personal material needs explicit structural boundaries, not just
  good intentions.

## 2026-03-27 — Pressure Testing Upgraded the Model

External pressure-test feedback did not just add ideas. It exposed where the
book was still too polite or too conceptual.

What changed:

- the contraction phase was elevated into the `AI Dip and Divergence Curve`
- overlapping knowledge-loss language was collapsed under `Knowledge Carriers`
- the playbook was forced into explicit Monday-morning modules
- `AuthorFlow` was made more visible as part of the book's underlying method

Method signal:
- Pressure testing is most useful when it turns vagueness into sharper structure
  rather than merely generating more labels.

## Emerging AuthorFlow Principles

- Separate thinking from drafting
- Preserve raw voice before polishing
- Keep collaborator context source-specific
- Use multiple models for triangulation, not authority
- Turn stories into transferable patterns
- Name concepts early, but only keep the ones that survive pressure
- Do not draft chapters until the claim is sharp
