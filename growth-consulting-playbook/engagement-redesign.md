# Engagement Redesign

> Playbook artifact for reshaping consulting engagements after the delivery
> economy weakens.

---

## Core Question

If the offer has changed, what has to change in scope, pricing, deliverables,
transfer, and measurement so the engagement does not collapse back into selling
hours?

---

## Redesign Moves

Use these moves to compare the old engagement with the new one:

1. outcomes instead of effort
2. transfer instead of dependency
3. leverage instead of labor
4. method instead of heroics
5. follow-on value instead of one-off completion

---

## Scope Questions

Ask these before finalizing an engagement:

1. What is the client actually buying: effort, output, capability, or a better
   operating result?
2. What part of the work should the firm still do directly?
3. What part of the work should be transferred into the client organization?
4. Where does AI create leverage inside the engagement?
5. What would make this engagement look successful while still leaving the
   client no stronger than before?
6. What follow-on value should this engagement naturally create?

---

## Before / After Lens

Old engagement logic:

- sell hours
- maximize staffed execution
- measure completion
- keep method mostly hidden
- let the client depend on the firm for repetition

Redesigned engagement logic:

- sell outcomes and leverage
- scope direct work deliberately
- transfer knowledge and method visibly
- measure stronger capability and better decisions
- create follow-on work at a higher level

---

## Reader Outcome

After using this tool, a consulting leader should be able to:

- redesign one engagement around outcomes, transfer, and leverage
- spot where an offer is being dragged back into the old delivery model
- rewrite success so the client becomes stronger, not just serviced
