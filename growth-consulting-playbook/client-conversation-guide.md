# Client Conversation Guide

> Playbook artifact for shifting AI conversations from reduction to growth.

---

## Core Question

How does a consulting leader reframe an AI conversation so the client moves
from "where can we cut?" to "where should this new capacity go?"

---

## Conversation Moves

Use these moves in sequence:

1. validate the efficiency instinct
2. expose the hidden loss
3. redirect to growth allocation
4. define what must be protected
5. propose a better engagement

---

## Core Questions to Ask

- What part of this work is genuinely getting cheaper?
- What would be lost if you took all of those gains as headcount reduction?
- Which people or functions are carrying continuity you cannot see on a
  spreadsheet?
- Where should the freed capacity go if the goal is growth rather than only
  savings?
- What would a successful first phase prove beyond cost reduction?

---

## Objections to Expect

### "We need hard ROI first."

Response direction:
- agree that proof matters
- show that shallow savings are not the only proof worth buying
- tie early proof to capability, continuity, and growth destination

### "This sounds softer than efficiency."

Response direction:
- reject the soft/hard framing
- position growth allocation and protected knowledge as more demanding, not less

### "We are not ready for a big transformation."

Response direction:
- lower the scope
- suggest a controlled first phase with clear outputs, transfer, and learning

---

## Reader Outcome

After using this tool, a consulting leader should be able to:

- reframe an AI conversation from reduction to growth
- challenge reduction-only logic without sounding naive
- open a better first engagement with a skeptical sponsor
