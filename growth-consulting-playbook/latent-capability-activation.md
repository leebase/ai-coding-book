# Latent Capability Activation

> Playbook artifact for surfacing underused people who may become far more
> valuable with AI leverage.

---

## Core Question

Who in the organization is being evaluated by their current output, but could
create much more value if given better leverage, broader problems, and AI-backed
 support?

This is not a replacement for performance management. It is a way to distinguish
underexpression from underperformance.

---

## Activation Signals

Look harder at people who consistently show one or more of these signals:

- strong judgment in a narrow role
- unusual curiosity across adjacent domains
- ability to explain complex things clearly
- repeated informal influence without formal authority
- high trust from peers or clients
- signs of frustration with repetitive work rather than inability to do it
- evidence that they improve quickly once context is given

---

## Discovery Questions

Ask managers or team leads:

1. Who solves better problems than their title suggests?
2. Who do people go to when the documented process stops helping?
3. Who produces unusually strong first passes when given more context?
4. Who seems underused rather than underpowered?
5. Who would likely create more value if routine work were reduced?
6. Who has range that the current role does not require?
7. Who gets better when the problem gets messier?

---

## Fast Exercise

Run this in a leadership or talent review session:

1. List 10-15 people below the obvious senior stars.
2. Mark who is currently judged mostly on throughput.
3. Mark who shows trust, synthesis, translation, or unusual judgment.
4. Ask where AI could remove repetitive burden from each person.
5. Identify 2-3 people to test in broader, AI-amplified work.

---

## Safe First Tests

Good activation tests:

- ask them to solve a problem one layer above their current role
- give them AI support plus broader context
- assign synthesis, redesign, or cross-functional translation work
- let them prototype a better workflow, artifact, or client-facing idea

Bad activation tests:

- just give them more of the same work
- confuse overload with growth
- expect immediate polish without support

---

## Distinguish Underexpression from Underperformance

Likely underexpression:

- better with context than with repetition
- strong pattern recognition
- rapid lift when friction drops
- high upside once leverage is added

Likely underperformance:

- weak judgment even with support
- repeated failure on core responsibilities
- no meaningful lift when friction is reduced

---

## Reader Outcome

After using this tool, a consulting leader should be able to:

- identify underused people who may become more valuable with AI leverage
- run a first-pass latent-capability discovery exercise
- choose a few safe activation experiments instead of making assumptions from
  role labels alone
