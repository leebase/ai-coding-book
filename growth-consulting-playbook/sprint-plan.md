## Planning Rules

- Do not start drafting chapters from scattered notes
- Do not lock the table of contents before the concept and framework work is done
- Do not let research accumulation replace selection
- Do not let the voice drift into prestige-consulting prose
- Review major planning outputs through the three audience lenses:
  - Big Consulting
  - mid-market consulting
  - the individual consultant

---

## Definition of Ready for Drafting

The book is ready for chapter drafting when:

1. `product-definition.md` is stable enough to guide tradeoffs
2. `book-concept.md` exists and is current
3. the signature framework is chosen or narrowed to a final short list
4. the audience and promise are explicit
5. the table of contents and chapter intents are written
6. the tone guardrails are explicit enough to prevent drift

Until then, the right work is synthesis, not prose.
