# Growth Allocation Decision

> Playbook artifact for deciding where AI-freed capacity should go.

---

## Core Question

When an AI initiative creates capacity, where should that capacity go so the
firm becomes stronger rather than merely leaner?

If the answer is vague, the productivity will usually disappear into shallow
savings, local overload relief, or margin smoothing.

---

## Allocation Categories

Use these categories explicitly:

1. More output
2. Deeper client work
3. New services
4. New markets
5. Internal capability

Do not assume all productivity should go to the same place.

---

## First-Pass Decision Questions

Ask these in sequence:

1. What capacity has actually been created?
2. Is that capacity repeatable or temporary?
3. Which growth constraint matters most right now?
4. Which allocation category best addresses that constraint?
5. What work will stop so the new allocation becomes real?
6. Who owns the reinvestment outcome?
7. What will we measure 90 days from now to know the allocation is working?

---

## Category Guidance

### More Output

Use when:
- demand already exists
- backlog is healthy
- quality can hold

Watch for:
- more volume without better economics
- people staying busy but nothing strategic changing

### Deeper Client Work

Use when:
- the firm has trust but not enough share of problem space
- teams can move upstream into diagnosis, redesign, or advisory

Watch for:
- simply doing more unpaid thinking
- more effort without stronger client dependence

### New Services

Use when:
- repeated client need is visible
- current work can be reframed into a more valuable offer

Watch for:
- renaming the old service without changing the outcome
- adding offers faster than the firm can deliver them well

### New Markets

Use when:
- the firm has portable capability
- sales reach, delivery model, and proof can travel

Watch for:
- expansion before the offer is sharp
- chasing territory without a differentiated angle

### Internal Capability

Use when:
- the firm lacks the infrastructure to sustain the shift
- knowledge capture, enablement, or workflow redesign is the real bottleneck

Watch for:
- endless internal improvement with no external payoff
- capability work that never changes revenue or client value

---

## Failure Signals

The allocation is weak if:

- no one can say where the productivity went
- utilization improved but growth did not
- managers quietly refill the time with old work
- AI output rises but offer quality does not
- the capacity gain is celebrated once and then disappears into the baseline

---

## Reader Outcome

After using this tool, a consulting leader should be able to:

- reject AI initiatives with no strategic destination
- choose a primary allocation path for freed capacity
- assign ownership and a near-term success signal to that choice
