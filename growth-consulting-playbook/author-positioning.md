## Core Positioning

Lee Harrington is not writing this book as a managing partner defending a
consulting résumé from the top of the house.

He is writing it from more than three decades spent inside consulting systems
across global firms, boutiques, startups, and client-side transformations. His
work has spanned architecture, delivery leadership, presales, practice
building, commercialization, training, and the creation of centers of
excellence.

That vantage point matters for this book because the argument is not mainly
about how to run partner compensation. It is about what consulting firms reward,
what they misprice, what they cut too easily, and what becomes more valuable
when AI changes the economics of delivery.

## Best Framing for "Why This Author"

Lee Harrington has spent more than three decades inside the consulting economy
in roles that sat close to the very tensions this book describes: architect,
delivery lead, presales leader, practice builder, commercialization lead, and
builder of centers of excellence. He has worked across global firms, boutiques,
startup environments, and client-side transformation efforts, which gives him a
 clear view of how consulting organizations actually create value, where they
 over-rely on visible labor, and what they cut too easily when pressure rises.

He is not writing as someone who ran a global consulting firm. He is writing as
someone who has spent a career inside consulting systems, watching what they
reward, what they undervalue, and what changes when one capable practitioner
can suddenly operate with far more leverage through AI. That makes him a useful
guide for a book about the migration of value inside consulting, not just the
management of consulting firms.

## Calibration Rule for the Manuscript

When the manuscript makes owner-level recommendations, it should speak from
pattern recognition, lived proximity, and decision logic rather than pretending
Lee personally executed every managing-partner move it recommends.

The most credible stance is:

- clear about the decision
- honest about the tradeoff
- grounded in lived consulting reality
- supported by story and observation rather than inflated persona
