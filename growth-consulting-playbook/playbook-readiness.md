# Playbook Readiness Check

> Pressure test for Chapters 5-12.
>
> Standard: after each chapter, the reader must be able to **do** something
> concrete they could not do before. If the output is only a better framework or
> clearer understanding, the chapter is still in argument mode rather than
> playbook mode.

---

## Why This Check Exists

The current manuscript has done the work of earning the playbook. The risk now
is not pacing. The risk is under-delivering on prescription after building up
the need for it.

The question for every remaining chapter is:

**What can a consulting leader do differently next week because of this
chapter?**

---

## Current Judgment

The current chapter sequence is still right.

What needs sharpening is not chapter order, but chapter output. Chapters 5-12
must produce reusable tools, decisions, diagnostics, operating questions,
conversation moves, and design patterns.

The book will earn `Playbook` in the title if these chapters become
action-bearing, not merely insight-bearing.

## Pressure Test: Remaining Chapters

## Chapter 5 — Who AI Replaces, Who It Multiplies

### Current Job

Reframe talent value after AI.

### Risk

This chapter could drift into smart commentary about role change without giving
the reader a way to classify actual people or roles inside their firm.

### Reader Must Be Able To Do

- sort roles into likely categories:
  - compress
  - augment
  - multiply
  - protect
- identify likely `Knowledge Carriers`
- spot where visible productivity understates carried value

### Chapter Must Deliver

- a role-mapping diagnostic
- a first-pass `Do Not Cut` screen
- warning signs that a role looks redundant but is carrying hidden continuity

### Test

If a reader cannot use this chapter in a talent review, it is not yet a
playbook chapter.

## Chapter 7 — Every Engagement Needs a Growth Thesis

### Current Job

Force the reader to answer where the productivity goes.

### Risk

This chapter could become a strong essay about reinvestment without producing a
decision method.

### Reader Must Be Able To Do

- test an AI initiative for whether it has a real growth thesis
- decide where freed capacity should go
- reject productivity gains that have no strategic destination

### Chapter Must Deliver

- the `Growth Allocation Decision`
- clear allocation categories:
  - more output
  - deeper client work
  - new services
  - new markets
  - internal capability
- signals that allocation is working
- signals that capacity is disappearing into shallow savings

### Test

If a partner meeting cannot use this chapter to decide where AI gains go, the
book has not yet earned `Playbook`.

## Production Rule Going Forward

Before drafting any chapter from Chapter 5 onward, answer these three prompts:

1. What can the reader do next week because of this chapter?
2. What artifact, tool, diagnostic, or decision process does the chapter give
   them?
3. What would make this chapter feel usable rather than merely insightful?

If those answers are weak, the chapter should be designed further before prose
is drafted.
