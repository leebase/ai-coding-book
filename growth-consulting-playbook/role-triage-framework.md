# Role Triage Framework

> First explicit playbook artifact for the back half of the book.
>
> Purpose: help consulting leaders sort roles and people after AI pressure
> without confusing visible productivity with actual strategic value.

---

## Core Question

When AI changes the economics of a role, should this role or person be:

- `Compress`
- `Augment`
- `Multiply`
- `Protect`

This is not a compensation framework and not a headcount-cutting formula.
It is a management decision aid for seeing where value is moving.

---

## The Four Buckets

## 1. Compress

Use this for work that is:

- routine
- bounded
- repeatable
- legible
- weakly tied to trust, judgment, or continuity

Typical signs:

- the output is easy to benchmark
- the task is mostly first-draft or first-pass work
- AI reliably reduces the human labor required
- the role's value is mainly throughput

Risk:

- confusing `Compress` with `Eliminate all people`
- removing apprenticeship entirely instead of redesigning it

---

## 2. Augment

Use this for work that is still needed, but can now be done faster or with
better range when supported by AI.

Typical signs:

- human judgment still matters
- AI improves speed, coverage, or responsiveness
- the role remains important, but the workflow should change

Examples:

- delivery leads
- analysts
- solution architects
- account managers

Manager action:

- redesign workflow
- raise expectations
- change measures of value

---

## 3. Multiply

Use this for people whose value expands materially when AI increases their
reach.

Typical signs:

- strong judgment
- strong synthesis ability
- high trust
- ability to work across functions
- ability to turn leverage into new value, not just faster output

Examples:

- top translators between business and technical teams
- client-trust owners
- internal capability builders
- high-context operators who can scale their judgment through AI

Manager action:

- give leverage intentionally
- expand scope
- support reuse and systemization
- direct freed capacity toward growth

---

## 4. Protect

Use this for people whose visible output understates their strategic value.

These are often `Knowledge Carriers`.

Typical signs:

- they hold continuity others rely on
- they stabilize relationships
- they prevent repeated mistakes
- they are central to apprenticeship or cross-functional coherence
- their departure would create confusion faster than the dashboard would predict

Manager action:

- do not cut on visible productivity alone
- identify what they carry
- transfer and preserve knowledge deliberately
- reduce fragility around their role without pretending they are replaceable now

---

## Diagnostic Questions

Ask these for any role or person under pressure:

1. Is the value mainly in throughput, or in judgment?
2. How much of the role is routine and legible?
3. How much of the role depends on trust, continuity, or translation?
4. If this person left tomorrow, what would break first?
5. Would AI make this person cheaper, or more powerful?
6. Is the work compressing, or is the person becoming more leverageable?
7. Are we measuring visible output while missing carried value?

---

## Warning Signs of Misclassification

You are likely misclassifying a role if:

- it looks redundant only because its best work appears as fewer problems
- it sits in the middle and therefore seems ordinary
- people rely on it informally more than formally
- the role has weak dashboard visibility but high interruption visibility
- the person is constantly pulled into exceptions, repairs, or translation work

---

## First-Pass Do Not Cut Screen

Pause before any reduction if the person or role:

- owns key client trust
- translates across business and technical teams
- carries undocumented context
- acts as an apprenticeship node
- catches expensive mistakes early
- holds continuity across multiple moving parts

If two or more of those are true, default to `Protect` until proven otherwise.

---

## How To Use This In Practice

1. List the roles or people under review.
2. Sort each one into a provisional bucket.
3. Highlight anyone who may be both `Augment` and `Protect`.
4. Flag anyone whose role looks compressible but whose person carries continuity.
5. Review the list with delivery, client, and capability leaders together.
6. Only then decide redesign, redeploy, or reduction.

---

## Intended Reader Outcome

After using this framework, a consulting leader should be able to:

- see where AI compresses work versus multiplies people
- avoid cutting hidden-value roles by accident
- begin a real `Do Not Cut List`
- make talent decisions that align with the Multiplier Model
