# Chapter Outline

> Working table of contents and chapter intent notes.

---

## Introduction — The Wrong Win

**Job:** Open with the central tension. Show why the obvious AI success can also
be the beginning of a longer-term failure.

**Must do:**
- establish the Efficiency Trap
- introduce the AI Dip and Divergence Curve
- frame the book as a strategic playbook, not an AI trend book
- tell the reader what kind of consulting leader this book is for

---

## Part I — The Trap

### Chapter 1 — The Delivery Economy Is Ending

**Job:** Show that software delivery and implementation labor are being
repriced.

**Must do:**
- explain why this is structural, not cyclical
- separate delivery pressure from total consulting collapse
- make the threat feel real without hype

### Chapter 2 — The Easy Win

**Job:** Explain why AI-driven cost reduction is so tempting.

**Must do:**
- show why executives and consultants both reach for cuts first
- make the logic understandable before critiquing it
- make the contraction phase explicit rather than implied

### Chapter 3 — What Gets Lost When You Cut Too Deep

**Job:** Make knowledge, continuity, and tacit judgment visible.

**Must do:**
- introduce Knowledge Carriers
- show that layoffs remove more than salary cost
- connect this to client trust and future growth
- begin the logic of the `Do Not Cut List`

### Act I Reflection — The Divine Struggle

**Job:** Widen the stakes beyond economics.

**Must do:**
- show AI as infrastructure for deeper human articulation, not just output speed
- remind the reader that the book is about what people may become with leverage,
  not only what firms may save

---

## Part II — The Reframe

### Chapter 4 — The Multiplier Model

**Job:** Introduce the central strategic fork.

**Must do:**
- contrast Substitution Road and Empowerment Road
- show 12- to 36-month consequences of each path
- make this the book's signature model

### Chapter 5 — Who AI Replaces, Who It Multiplies

**Job:** Reframe talent value after AI.

**Must do:**
- show which roles are compressed
- show which capabilities rise in value
- make Knowledge Carriers memorable

### Chapter 6 — The People You Didn't Know You Had

**Job:** Bring in the human-amplification layer.

**Must do:**
- argue that AI exposes hidden capability
- use selective lived examples
- connect empowerment to underused talent, not just efficiency

### Chapter 7 — Every Engagement Needs a Growth Thesis

**Job:** Force the reader to answer where the productivity goes.

**Must do:**
- introduce the Growth Reinvestment Loop
- introduce the Growth Allocation Decision
- give leaders a way to test whether an engagement is actually strategic

### Act II Reflection — The Songs

**Job:** Show that multiplication is not limited to enterprise productivity.

**Must do:**
- use songwriting as proof of expression amplified rather than voice replaced
- strengthen the human-amplification claim without drifting into memoir

---

## Part III — The Playbook

### Chapter 8 — Stop Selling the Wrong Work

**Job:** Clarify what consulting firms need to stop relying on.

**Must do:**
- identify commodity delivery patterns
- show what becomes harder to defend as a premium offer

### Chapter 9 — Build the New Service Portfolio

**Job:** Show what growth-oriented consulting offerings look like.

**Must do:**
- reposition around capability building, workflow redesign, knowledge
  preservation, and human-AI operating design
- keep the advice usable for mid-market firms
- map major service moves to concrete playbook modules

### Chapter 10 — Redesign the Engagement

**Job:** Show how pricing, scope, deliverables, and success metrics change.

**Must do:**
- move beyond hour sale logic
- emphasize outcomes, transfer, and longer-term value
- make `Engagement Redesign` an explicit playbook pattern

### Chapter 11 — Talent and Operating Model

**Job:** Describe what kind of firm can actually deliver this.

**Must do:**
- address who matters more after AI
- cover apprenticeship, judgment, and internal operating discipline
- connect to AuthorFlow-like internal systems where relevant
- make the `Do Not Cut List` concrete

### Chapter 12 — The Client Conversation

**Job:** Give the reader language for reframing the conversation.

**Must do:**
- move from efficiency to growth
- address objections
- help the reader sell the better path

### Act III Reflection — The Eternal Now

**Job:** Signal that the reader is also a person with unreached capacity.

**Must do:**
- use the story as a closing expansion of the book's promise
- connect consulting transformation to personal intellectual enlargement
- leave the reader with a larger sense of what leverage can unlock

---

## Part IV — The Firms That Win

### Chapter 13 — The New Consulting Firm

**Job:** Synthesize what a winning post-delivery firm looks like.

**Must do:**
- describe the emerging business model
- show how firms differ by scale
- end with a realistic but compelling picture of the future

---

## Conclusion — Don't Cut Your Way to the Future

**Job:** Close on the central warning and the central invitation.

**Must do:**
- restate the trap
- restate the opportunity
- leave the reader with a strategic choice, not a slogan

---

## First Chapter Recommendation

**Draft first:** `Introduction — The Wrong Win`

### Why

- it forces the voice and thesis to work together
- it sets the register for the whole book
- it reveals quickly whether the book sounds human enough
- it creates a reusable opening argument for proposals, talks, and positioning
- it lets us test the AI Dip and Divergence Curve immediately
