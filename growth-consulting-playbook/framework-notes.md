# Framework Notes

> Current framework judgment for the book.

---

## Recommended Framework Stack

The book should not try to make every good phrase into a full framework.

The cleanest stack right now is:

1. **The Efficiency Trap**
   - the problem
2. **The AI Dip and Divergence Curve**
   - the macro visual
3. **The Multiplier Model**
   - the strategic fork
4. **The Growth Reinvestment Loop**
   - the operational answer
5. **Knowledge Carriers**
   - the human-value lens

---

## 1. The Efficiency Trap

### Job

Name the central mistake.

### Core Idea

AI makes delivery cheaper. Leaders harvest the savings through cuts. The short-
term logic is clear. The long-term damage is harder to see until continuity,
judgment, and growth capacity are already weaker.

### Why It Stays

- clearest tension in the book
- feels human and memorable
- likely strongest recurring phrase

---

## 2. The AI Dip and Divergence Curve

### Job

Make the contraction phase explicit and show that the real difference appears
after the first wave of AI pressure.

### Core Idea

The market enters a shared dip:

- delivery economics weaken
- firms cut
- knowledge is lost
- false efficiency wins appear

Then firms diverge:

- **Reduction path** -> shallow savings, weaker continuity, slower long-term growth
- **Reinvestment path** -> redesign, stronger capability, better growth options

### Why It Matters

- It adds the missing contraction phase
- It explains why the first AI wins can be misleading
- It works as a keynote visual, a chapter visual, and a cover-adjacent concept

---

## 3. The Multiplier Model

### Job

Serve as the book's main strategic diagram.

### Core Idea

One AI productivity decision opens two roads:

- **Substitution Road**
  - use AI to remove labor
  - capture cost savings
  - risk knowledge loss, weaker continuity, and reduced future surface area

- **Empowerment Road**
  - use AI to multiply people
  - redesign work
  - redirect gains into growth, stronger capability, and new value

### Why It Wins Right Now

- It creates a visible fork
- It turns philosophy into choice
- It can structure multiple chapters
- It works for firms and for individuals

---

## 4. The Growth Reinvestment Loop

### Job

Answer the practical question:

**Where does the productivity go?**

### Draft Loop

1. create productivity
2. protect context and knowledge
3. reinvest freed capacity
4. build new capability or new offers
5. expand revenue, resilience, or client value

### Why It Matters

Without this loop, "growth" stays aspirational.

---

## 5. Knowledge Carriers

### Job

Give the book a human and strategic lens on talent.

### Core Idea

The most valuable people after AI may not be the visibly busiest or most
technically productive. They may be the people who hold context, continuity,
relationships, trust, and judgment that the organization cannot easily
reconstruct.

### Why It Matters

- turns knowledge loss into something visible
- supports the argument against indiscriminate cuts
- can travel outside consulting into broader management relevance

### Language Decision

`Knowledge Carriers` is now the dominant phrase.

Use other labels like `Knowledge Drain`, `AI Amnesia`, or `Hollow Enterprise`
only if they add something distinct in a specific passage. Do not let them
compete with the core term.

---

## Three Core Playbook Modules

Every major concept should map to a Monday-morning move.

### 1. The Do Not Cut List

Protect:

- Knowledge Carriers
- relationship owners
- cross-functional translators
- apprenticeship nodes

### 2. The Growth Allocation Decision

When AI frees capacity, choose explicitly:

- more output
- deeper client work
- new services
- new markets
- internal capability building

### 3. Engagement Redesign

Change:

- pricing logic
- deliverables
- transfer expectations
- measures of value

---

## What To De-Emphasize

- Too many overlapping labels for knowledge loss
- framework names that sound like analyst branding
- any concept that duplicates the Multiplier Model without adding a new
  decision or distinction

---

## Visual Recommendation

The primary diagram system should now be:

1. **AI Dip and Divergence Curve**
   - shared dip -> reduction path / reinvestment path
2. **Multiplier Model**
   - the strategic fork inside the divergence

Then supporting sidebars or small visuals can explain:

- the Growth Reinvestment Loop
- Knowledge Carriers

That keeps the book from feeling framework-drunk.
