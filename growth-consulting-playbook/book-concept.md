## Working Title

**The Growth Consulting Playbook: How Firms Grow When Software Delivery No Longer Pays the Bills**

---

## One-Sentence Thesis

As AI drives down the value of software delivery labor, the consulting firms
that win will be the ones that turn productivity gains into client growth and
human capability instead of treating AI primarily as a headcount-reduction tool.

---

## Short Thesis

Consulting is entering a structural shift. The old model of monetizing coding
hours, implementation labor, and delivery-heavy transformation work is under
pressure as AI makes technical production faster, cheaper, and more abundant.

The first move many firms and clients will make is the obvious one: use AI to
cut labor cost.

This book argues that this is often the wrong strategic move.

It may improve short-term efficiency, but it can also destroy institutional
knowledge, continuity, apprenticeship, and future growth capacity. Consulting
firms that help clients optimize only for reduction risk shrinking their
clients' long-term potential and their own future relevance.

The stronger play is to use AI to amplify people, redesign work, retain the
capabilities that matter most, and redirect productivity gains into growth.

The strongest defensible version of this claim is not that all software
delivery stops mattering. It is that **routine, bounded, repeatable delivery
work is under the greatest pressure**, and that this is enough to force a
strategic shift in the consulting business model.

---

## What This Book Is Really About

This is not mainly a book about AI tools.

It is a book about:

- the collapse of labor-as-value
- the rise of judgment, orchestration, and capability-as-value
- the strategic danger of confusing efficiency with growth
- the hidden cost of cutting too deeply
- the underused human capacity AI can help unlock

---

## Core Tension

### The Efficiency Trap

The logic is easy to understand:

1. AI makes delivery cheaper
2. clients want savings
3. consultants can sell those savings
4. everyone gets rewarded in the short term

But the same move can:

- cut knowledge
- weaken continuity
- erode apprenticeship
- shrink growth capacity
- commoditize the consulting firm that led the cut

This is the book's central tension.

### The AI Dip and Divergence Curve

The book should make one more thing explicit:

there is likely a contraction phase first.

The early AI era does not begin with clean, compounding growth. It begins with
confusion, false wins, labor compression, knowledge loss, and sharp pressure on
old delivery economics.

Then the paths diverge:

- **Reduction path**
  - cut labor
  - capture shallow savings
  - weaken continuity and future capability

- **Reinvestment path**
  - preserve critical context
  - redesign work
  - redirect capacity into new offerings, deeper client value, and growth

This curve is now the leading candidate for the book's signature visual because
it combines:

- the dip
- the fork
- the strategic consequence

## The Reader

### Primary Reader

The primary reader is a consulting leader who can still change the shape of the
firm:

- founder
- managing partner
- practice leader
- consulting business builder
- transformation leader inside a consulting firm

The current best fit is **boutique-to-mid-market consulting leadership**, while
keeping the book legible to larger firms.

### Secondary Readers

- leaders in larger consulting firms
- enterprise buyers of consulting services
- internal transformation or strategy leaders
- independent consultants repositioning their own practice

### Three Audience Lenses We Must Preserve

The book should remain reviewable through these three lenses:

1. Big Consulting
2. mid-market consulting
3. the individual consultant

The book cannot quietly overfit one of these and still claim to be a playbook.

---

## Reader Shift

### What the Reader Believes at the Start

- AI is changing consulting, but the safe move is still efficiency
- software delivery may get cheaper, but good firms will just "do more with less"
- headcount reduction is the cleanest proof of AI value
- AI strategy is mainly a tooling and pricing problem

### What the Reader Should Believe by the End

- delivery compression changes the consulting business model itself
- cost reduction alone is an incomplete and often dangerous AI strategy
- the most valuable post-AI capabilities are judgment, context, orchestration,
  relationships, and growth design
- firms need explicit plans for where productivity goes
- the next consulting moat is not just AI adoption, but human amplification plus
  operating-system design

---

## Why This Book Is Different

This book's strongest differentiator is the **reflexive move**:

Most thought leadership tells consulting firms how to help clients adopt AI.
This book asks what consulting firms must become when AI attacks their own
delivery economics.

That creates four distinct angles:

1. apply the automation-vs-augmentation debate to the consulting firm itself
2. connect AI productivity to knowledge loss and continuity risk
3. argue for growth reinvestment instead of reduction-only logic
4. translate all of that into a practical playbook for firms that are not
   McKinsey-sized

It is best understood as a **migration-of-value** argument, not a
"consulting-is-dead" argument.

## Tonal Intent

The voice must be:

- human
- reflective
- inviting
- sharp
- grounded
- gently contrarian

It must not sound like:

- prestige-consulting prose
- a McKinsey wannabe book
- a generic management keynote
- a consultant trying to sound inevitable

The authority should come from lived recognition and clear thought, not
institutional tone.

## Anchor Stories

### Top-Tier Anchor Stories

1. **GitHub Copilot Day / "Walking in and playing the Rach 2"**
   - strongest proof of AI as expert multiplier

2. **Saving Don / Knowledge Carrier Forensics**
   - strongest proof that a person's value is often context, continuity, and
     relationship judgment rather than visible output

3. **Lewis and Clark AI Expedition Proposal**
   - strong example of strategic output compressed by AI while the human still
     provides the real framing

4. **AgentFlow as a Personal COE**
   - strong bridge from individual practice to consulting operating model

### Strong Supporting Stories

- GPLee and the annual reviews
- Saturday in the life of the AI enhanced
- Coding clarity under fire
- the Book Factory / multi-agent writing system
- Snowflake architect escaping the trap

### Human-Flourishing Stories

These are strategically valuable, but should be used selectively and only where
they deepen the central argument.

- The Divine Struggle
- The Eternal Now
- the songs

These matter because they prove that AI can amplify human expression and latent
capability, not just productivity.

### Placement Decision

These should **not** become chapter anchors.

Current decision:

- use **one human-flourishing story per act**
- place them as **act closers or reflective pivots**
- let them widen the stakes without turning the book into memoir

Current working placement:

- **The Divine Struggle** closes the early trap material by reframing what is at
  stake beyond economics
- **the songs / songwriting** close the reframe by showing that AI multiplies
  more than enterprise productivity
- **The Eternal Now** closes the playbook/future material by reminding the
  reader that the consulting leader is also a person with unreached capacity

These are no longer optional color. They are now treated as **supporting proof
that AI expands human capability beyond role boundaries**.

### Story Rule

Stories must be used as **transferable patterns**, not as autobiography.

Each story should answer:

1. what happened
2. what changed
3. what principle it proves
4. how the reader could apply the pattern

## What the Research Currently Supports

### Strongly Supported

- AI is putting structural pressure on delivery-heavy consulting economics
- augmentation often produces better outcomes than naive automation
- AI productivity gains are real but uneven
- many firms are using AI in ways that affect headcount
- knowledge loss and tacit knowledge erosion are real organizational risks
- the strongest opportunity is not only efficiency, but operating-model redesign
- the market story is better described as value migration than industry death

### Strongly Supported but Needs Exact Citation Discipline

- McKinsey's reported 20-30% contraction in core tech services
- McKinsey's reported $100B-$400B new value pools
- METR's result that experienced developers were slower with AI while believing
  they were faster
- BCG / Harvard evidence for AI as a skill-leveling augmentor

### Supported Indirectly, but Still Needs Care

- consulting firms that optimize for cuts are shrinking their own future market
- growth reinvestment outperforms reduction-first strategy in consulting
- the most strategically valuable employees after AI are often context carriers,
  not obvious high-output specialists

### Biggest Evidence Gaps

- mid-market consulting case studies
- consulting-specific knowledge-loss examples after AI-led cuts
- concrete examples of freed capacity being redirected into growth
- how firms below Big Consulting scale are changing pricing and service
  portfolio design

## Current Positioning

### Best Current Position

This is a **strategy and repositioning book for consulting leaders**.

It is not:

- an AI trends book
- a prompt book
- a generic future-of-work book
- a memoir
- a technical guide to coding tools

### Best Current Promise

The book helps consulting leaders understand:

- what is actually under threat
- what is becoming more valuable
- what they must stop selling
- what they must start building
- how to turn AI productivity into a growth strategy rather than a shrinking
  strategy

### Playbook Commitment

To earn the word *playbook*, every major concept in the manuscript should map
to an action pattern.

At minimum, the book now commits to three practical modules:

1. **The Do Not Cut List**
   - protect Knowledge Carriers, relationship owners, cross-functional
     translators, and apprenticeship nodes
2. **The Growth Allocation Decision**
   - decide explicitly where freed capacity goes
3. **Engagement Redesign**
   - rework scope, pricing, deliverables, and success metrics for the
     post-delivery economy

### Best Current "Why Now"

The delivery economy is being repriced faster than most consulting firms are
repositioning.

## Current Best Summary Paragraph

*The Growth Consulting Playbook* argues that as AI drives down the value of
software delivery labor, consulting firms face a dangerous temptation: use the
gains for labor reduction and call it transformation. That move can improve
short-term efficiency, but it can also destroy the knowledge, continuity, and
human leverage that make long-term growth possible. The firms that win will be
the ones that use AI to amplify people, redesign work, protect context, and
turn productivity gains into new capability and new growth rather than simply
smaller payrolls.
