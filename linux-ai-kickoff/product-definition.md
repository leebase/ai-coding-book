# Product Definition: Teach Yourself Anything

> What this book is, who it is for, and what it is trying to unlock.

---

## The Book in One Sentence

A practical guide that helps a motivated young person learn how to teach
themselves almost anything with AI, a Linux machine, official manuals, and real
projects.

---

## Working Title

**Teach Yourself Anything**

Subtitle working line:
**A Real-World Guide to AI, Linux, and Self-Directed Learning**

---

## The Real Subject

This is not mainly a Linux book.

This is not mainly an AI book.

It is a book about human agency.

Linux and AI are the two foundational tools that open the rest of the doors.
The reader is learning how to direct tools, learn from manuals, build
confidence through action, and create their own path instead of waiting for one
to be handed to them.

---

## The Reader

### Primary persona

The capable kid without a map.

They are:

- smart but not on a clean academic track
- short on money, structure, or both
- carrying real-world responsibility
- curious, but not yet pointed in a direction
- ready to work, but unsure where to begin

They do not need to be convinced they are broken. They need a path, a system,
and proof that they can do hard things.

---

## The Problem

The problem is not lack of intelligence.

The problem is not lack of access to information.

The real problems are:

- no clear path
- no habit of self-direction
- no proof, yet, that they can figure things out
- no framework for turning AI into leverage instead of noise

---

## The Thesis

The future does not belong to AI. It belongs to humans who learn to use AI
effectively.

AI is not the point of the book. The reader is. AI is one of the tools that
lets a motivated person move faster, learn better, and open doors that used to
stay closed.

---

## Core Mental Models

### 1. AI as a team you direct

Do not use AI like a vending machine. Use it like a team you direct.

The book adapts Lee's executive model into a reader-friendly form:

1. Pick the right hero for the job
2. Give them the backstory
3. Give them a specific mission
4. Stay in charge and correct the work

### 2. AI is more like a horse than a car

GenAI is not a deterministic machine in the way a car is. It is more like a
horse: powerful, useful, responsive, and opinionated. It has tendencies. It
needs direction. If you stop paying attention, it drifts.

The reader's job is not to press the perfect button. The reader's job is to
learn how to guide.

### 3. Manual first, AI second

The reader should learn to use official manuals and primary sources instead of
outsourcing all understanding to AI.

For Omarchy, the primary technical companion is:

- [The Omarchy Manual](https://learn.omacom.io/2/the-omarchy-manual)

The pattern is:

1. Read the relevant section of the manual
2. Ask AI to explain what is unclear
3. Try the step on the machine
4. Observe what happened
5. Repeat

---

## What the Book Uses

### The machine

- A pre-configured Omarchy Linux machine
- AI tools available on-device
- Browser access to leading AI systems

### The method

- AI as tutor, coach, and collaborator
- short missions with visible outcomes
- repetition, correction, and reflection
- manuals and docs as first-class tools

### The book

- gives the missions
- frames the stakes
- keeps the reader moving
- turns small wins into durable confidence

---

## Core Principles

1. You learn by doing, not by waiting.
2. AI is a teacher and lever, not a shortcut.
3. Small wins build confidence.
4. You are allowed to try things before you feel ready.
5. Reading the manual is a power move, not a punishment.
6. Motivation grows when the work opens real doors.

---

## Structure

### Part 1 - You're Not Stuck

- possibility, dignity, and agency
- what this machine changes
- what AI can and cannot do for you

### Part 2 - Meet Your Machine

- Omarchy orientation
- keyboard-first navigation
- terminal and file basics
- using the manual on purpose

### Part 3 - Meet Your Teacher

- how to use AI well
- how to ask for explanation instead of just answers
- how to stay in charge of the work

### Part 4 - Do Things That Matter

- small real projects
- automation
- problem solving
- stacking wins into skill

### Part 5 - Now You Choose

- choosing a direction
- building a learning path
- using the tools beyond tech-only outcomes

---

## Constraints

- No academic tone
- No hype about AI replacing people
- No shame-based motivation
- No long theory sections without a mission
- No chapter that lacks a visible outcome
- Must work for readers on modest hardware and limited budget

---

## Success Criteria

When the reader finishes the book, they should:

1. feel more capable than when they started
2. be comfortable navigating and using their Linux machine
3. know how to use AI as a tutor instead of a crutch
4. know how to read a manual and extract the next useful step
5. have completed real tasks they can point to
6. believe they can keep learning without waiting for permission

---

## Out of Scope

- turning the reader into a Linux administrator
- exhaustive AI model comparisons
- advanced programming theory
- abstract motivation with no practical work attached
- pretending AI gives exact, deterministic output like a traditional program
