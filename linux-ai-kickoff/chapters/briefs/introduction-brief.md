# Introduction Brief

## Mission

- Mission: Reframe the reader from "stuck person on the outside" to "capable
  person who can learn to use these tools."
- Why it matters: If the introduction does not create belief and momentum, the
  rest of the book will feel like instructions instead of a path.
- Success looks like: The reader feels seen, understands what the book is
  really about, and creates a short note about the doors they want to open.
- Next door it opens: Part 1 can start from agency instead of from despair or
  abstraction.

## Purpose

The introduction needs to do six things:

1. Meet the reader with dignity
2. State that Linux and AI are tools, not the true subject
3. Establish the thesis that the future belongs to humans who learn to use AI
   effectively
4. Preview the Team-Captain and Horse-Not-Car models without over-explaining
   them
5. Explain how to use the book
6. Give the reader one tiny action to take immediately

## What To Keep In

- The reader is not behind, broken, or too late
- This book is about self-direction
- AI is a tutor and force multiplier, not destiny
- The machine matters because it opens doors
- Action beats passive inspiration

## What To Keep Out

- long technical explanations
- Linux jargon
- AI model comparisons
- anything that sounds like startup hype
- guilt-based motivation
