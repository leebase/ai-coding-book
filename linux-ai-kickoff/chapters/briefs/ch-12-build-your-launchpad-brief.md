# Chapter 12 Brief - Build Your Launchpad

## Mission

- Mission: Start Part 4 by helping the reader create one real workspace they
  keep, tied to one goal they actually care about.
- Why it matters: Part 3 taught the mental models and the working loop. Now the
  book needs proof in the real world, not just better thinking.
- Success looks like: The reader creates a launchpad folder for one real goal
  and puts a plain-language mission note inside it.
- Next door it opens: Chapter 13 can turn that launchpad into leverage with one
  useful helper tool.

## Purpose

This chapter needs to:

1. shift from warm-up drills to a kept artifact
2. keep the goal personal and meaningful, not abstract
3. use the ask-refine-test-repeat loop on something real
4. stay technically simple enough for a beginner
5. end with a workspace the reader can keep building in

## Working Example Set

Primary artifact:

- `~/launchpad`
- one goal-specific subfolder
- one `mission.md` note with a short mission statement

Core moves:

1. choose one real direction
2. shrink it into a folder name and mission sentence
3. create the workspace
4. keep it

Keep out of chapter:

- giant productivity systems
- editor rabbit holes
- complicated shell setup
- multiple parallel goals
- turning the chapter into life-planning theater

## Working Outline

1. Why a launchpad matters
2. Choose one real goal worth giving a home
3. Use AI to shrink the goal into a folder and mission
4. Create the folder and note
5. End with a kept artifact and Part 4 momentum
