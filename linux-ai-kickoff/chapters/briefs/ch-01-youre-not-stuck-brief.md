# Chapter 1 Brief - You're Not Stuck

## Mission

- Mission: Move the reader from vague frustration to one concrete direction
  they can begin acting on.
- Why it matters: A reader who still feels generally trapped cannot use Linux,
  manuals, or AI well because every tool gets swallowed by overwhelm.
- Success looks like: The reader picks one real problem, goal, or door to move
  on instead of carrying around a cloud of vague wanting.
- Next door it opens: Chapter 2 can show what the machine gives them in service
  of that chosen direction.

## Purpose

This chapter needs to:

1. break the story that the reader is just "behind"
2. show that confusion and drift are often a map problem, not a character flaw
3. narrow the reader's attention to one thing worth moving on
4. make action feel smaller and more believable than they expected

## Keep In

- dignity
- directness
- ordinary real-life examples
- one visible written outcome

## Keep Out

- tech explanation
- fake inspiration
- "follow your dreams" vagueness
- career advice lists

## Working Outline

1. What "stuck" usually feels like
2. Why stuck is often a missing map, not a missing brain
3. The problem with trying to solve everything at once
4. Pick one door
5. Write it down in plain language
