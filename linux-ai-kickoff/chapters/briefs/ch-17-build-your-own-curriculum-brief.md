# Chapter 17 Brief - Build Your Own Curriculum

## Mission

- Mission: Help the reader turn `direction.md` into a small self-built
  curriculum they can actually use.
- Why it matters: A direction is encouraging, but it still needs structure. If
  the reader does not know what to do next, motivation drains away fast. This
  chapter should turn direction into a simple roadmap without making learning
  feel like school.
- Success looks like: The reader creates a kept `curriculum.md` note with a few
  missions, a few docs, and a few tools tied to one direction.
- Next door it opens: Chapter 18 can show how to keep moving without waiting
  for permission from the book, school, or anybody else.

## Purpose

This chapter needs to:

1. make "curriculum" feel practical, not academic
2. build directly from `direction.md`
3. show that manuals and docs still matter beyond Linux setup
4. keep AI in the role of coach and organizer
5. end with one roadmap the reader can actually start using

## Working Shape

Core moves:

1. define a tiny curriculum
2. choose a few missions, not a giant plan
3. choose a few primary docs or manuals
4. choose a few tools worth using
5. write `curriculum.md`

Possible artifact sections:

- direction
- three next missions
- two docs or manuals
- two tools
- first thing to do next

Keep out of chapter:

- academic language
- giant reading lists
- certification or credential talk
- perfect-plan pressure
- vague productivity advice
