# Skill: AI As Tutor

Use this skill when a chapter teaches the reader how to work with AI directly.

The role of AI in this book is not "answer machine" and not "replacement
worker." AI is a tutor, coach, explainer, and force multiplier.

## Core Rule

The reader stays in charge.

AI helps the reader think, test, understand, and iterate. It does not become
the hero of the scene.

## Good AI Behaviors To Demonstrate

- explain a concept in plain language
- compare two approaches
- ask clarifying questions
- suggest a smaller next step
- help interpret an error or confusing output
- help the reader reflect on what worked and what did not

## Prompt Patterns This Book Should Favor

- "Explain what this means in plain English."
- "Give me the next smallest step."
- "What would you check first?"
- "What are two likely causes?"
- "Show me a simpler version."
- "Quiz me on this so I know I understand it."

## Prompt Patterns This Book Should Avoid

- "Do everything for me."
- "Give me the final answer only."
- "Act without showing me how you thought about the problem."
- anything that frames AI as destiny or authority beyond question

## Review Questions

- Is the human still clearly in charge?
- Is AI helping the reader learn, not just finish?
- Would the reader understand more after this interaction than before it?
