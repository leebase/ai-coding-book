# Proof Map

> Story-to-chapter proof plan for *The Personal Empowerment with AI Handbook*.
>
> This file exists so the manuscript does not drift into either memoir or
> abstraction. It decides which lived stories are load-bearing, which are
> supporting, and which should stay out unless a chapter truly needs them.

---

## Proof Rules

- Every major chapter should have one anchor proof, not a pile of anecdotes.
- Anchor stories must prove a principle, not merely show that Lee is unusual.
- Supporting stories should widen transferability or add texture, not steal the
  chapter's center.
- If a story does not change the reader's trust in the chapter, it does not
  belong in the chapter.
- Story and application must stay separate: what happened, what it proves, and
  how the reader can use it are different jobs.

---

## Story Tiers

### Anchor Stories

These are the stories most likely to carry chapter-level weight.

1. **Withholding IP from Cognizant**
   - proves executive ownership, authorship, and responsibility
2. **The Devin Class**
   - proves thinking in dialogue rather than silence
3. **Writing Books Rapidly**
   - proves forged output over one-shot generation
4. **The Hockey Stick Bet**
   - proves expansion into a larger identity rather than safe optimization
5. **Music Creation**
   - proves curiosity becoming artifact
6. **Parenting Conversations**
   - proves reflection support without surrendering judgment
7. **The Divine Struggle**
   - proves deep inquiry and articulation without outsourcing belief
8. **Real Boy Moments**
   - proves the need to distinguish AI-side emergence from human-side authorship
9. **The Forge Itself**
   - proves repeated co-thinking as an operating practice, not a one-off trick

### Supporting Stories

These are strong, but should usually support rather than lead.

1. **Health and Fasting**
   - strong for responsibility and boundary language
2. **Am I Adding Value?**
   - useful for real-time executive posture and contribution discipline
3. **Meta Story: Expanded Identity**
   - strong for Introduction and Conclusion, weaker as a stand-alone chapter

### Hold / Use Sparingly

- Any story that only repeats the same point already proved by a stronger story
- Any story that requires too much Lee-specific context before it becomes
  useful
- Any story that sounds impressive but does not translate into reader action

## Story Gaps To Watch

- There is not yet a clean anchor story for an ordinary reader using AI in a
  small, non-exceptional way. The manuscript will need modest examples so the
  reader does not assume the book only applies to high-agency edge cases.
- Chapter 10 risks depending too much on caution language and not enough on a
  vivid weakening story. That chapter may need either a stronger lived example
  or a compact composite pattern.
- Chapter 6 currently leans on growth and value stories more than on a pure
  learning story. A clearer "learned this with AI without outsourcing the
  learning" example would strengthen it.

## Composite Everyday Example

Use a recurring modest example when a chapter needs accessibility more than
biographical proof.

Current best candidate:

- a manager preparing for a difficult performance review

Low-agency version:

- ask AI to write the script
- accept the language because it sounds smooth

High-agency version:

- ask AI to role-play likely reactions and objections
- test your own wording against resistance
- refine your thinking before the real conversation

Why it belongs:

- it is ordinary enough to feel reachable
- it makes the Executive Model concrete
- it shows reflection support without outsourcing judgment or empathy

## Current Recommendation

- Use one anchor story per chapter.
- Let supporting stories do only one extra job each.
- Keep the Introduction, Chapter 4, and Conclusion tied to the larger identity
  expansion arc.
- Keep Chapters 7-10 especially disciplined so the book stays useful and
  trustworthy in high-stakes domains.
