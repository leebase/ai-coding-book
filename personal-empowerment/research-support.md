# Research Support

> Reusable research support map for *The Personal Empowerment with AI Handbook*.
>
> This file normalizes the existing research memo into chapter-facing claims,
> confidence levels, cautions, and open gaps so drafting can use research
> precisely instead of dumping studies onto the page.

---

## Research Use Rules

- Research should support the book's argument, not replace its lived proof.
- Do not let a vivid statistic become doctrine until the exact source is
  verified and the study design is understood.
- Separate empirical support from analytical framing and opinion writing.
- Mark where the manuscript is making an inference beyond the sources.
- Prefer a few credible supports per chapter over a pile of weakly related
  references.

---

## Claim Tiers

### Strong Support

These are the safest themes to lean on once exact source checks are done.

1. **Cognitive offloading can weaken engagement and critical thinking**
   - support type: empirical plus analytical
   - use for: Introduction, Chapter 6, Chapter 10
2. **Extended / distributed cognition gives a credible frame for thinking with tools**
   - support type: analytical / theoretical
   - use for: Chapter 2, Chapter 11
3. **AI systems can become sycophantic, over-compliant, and overtrusted**
   - support type: analytical plus product-observation
   - use for: Chapter 10
4. **AI collaboration can increase volume without reliably increasing originality**
   - support type: empirical
   - use for: Chapter 3, Chapter 9

### Moderate Support

These are usable, but should be framed carefully.

1. **People are increasingly using AI for cognitive work, not just automation**
   - support type: usage analysis
   - use for: Introduction, Chapter 2
2. **AI can help with reflection, mental-health-adjacent support, and therapeutic alliance**
   - support type: early empirical plus design literature
   - use for: Chapter 7
   - caution: support is not the same as substitution for therapy or clinical care
3. **Dialectical, adversarial, and role-based prompting can produce better thought**
   - support type: analytical / practitioner pattern
   - use for: Chapter 2, Chapter 11
4. **Developmental foreclosure is a real concern for children and adolescents**
   - support type: analytical / expert warning
   - use for: Chapter 7, Chapter 10

### Weak / Use Sparingly

These may still be useful, but should not carry major argumentative weight.

1. **Polymath founder / prompting lawyer examples**
   - issue: anecdotal and adjacent to the book's real claim
2. **Generic future-of-work skill forecasts**
   - issue: broad and easy to overstate
3. **Environmental cost in this manuscript**
   - issue: real, but not central to the book's main argument unless a later
     section explicitly widens scope

## Original Claims The Book Owns

These are not things research will fully prove for us. They are the book's own
named contributions and should be presented as such.

1. **AI as Thinking Partner** as the preferred operating posture for personal empowerment
2. **The Executive Model** as the accountability frame
3. **The Empowerment Loop** as the repeatable practice model
4. **Forge, Don't Factory** as the craft discipline
5. **RBM / MoG** as a distinction between AI-side emergence and human-side authorship

Research can support the surrounding logic, but these specific frames are Lee's
contributions.

## Chapter Support Map

## Introduction — This Is Not About Going Faster

**Claim:** AI can make a person faster without making them stronger.

**Best support:**

- cognitive offloading / weakened critical-thinking concerns
- learning-versus-avoidance framing
- evidence that AI use is shifting toward cognitive labor

**Complication:**

- faster output is still real value, so the chapter must avoid sounding like it
  denies obvious benefits

**Confidence:** strong

**Use guidance:**

- short, high-value research reference near the hinge
- no study parade

## Biggest Evidence Gaps

1. The current memo needs stronger primary sourcing on the exact strength of
   the cognitive-offloading and critical-thinking claims before any numeric
   language goes into the manuscript.
2. Mental-health and therapeutic-alliance material should be treated as
   boundary-sensitive support, not broad permission to position AI as therapy.
3. There is not yet a strong primary source base for `RBM` / `MoG`, which is
   fine because they are original concepts, but the surrounding authorship
   discussion needs careful framing.
4. Several examples in the current memo are useful illustrations but not strong
   enough to function as central evidence.

---

## Drafting Recommendation

- Use research most heavily in the Introduction, Chapter 6, and Chapter 10.
- Use research lightly in Chapters 2, 3, 7, and 8.
- Let lived proof dominate Chapters 4, 5, 9, and 11.
- Treat all numeric claims as provisional until the exact primary source is
  re-checked before chapter drafting.
