# Framework Notes

> Current framework judgment for the book.

---

## Recommended Framework Stack

This book should not try to make every good phrase into a full framework.

The cleanest stack right now is:

1. **AI as Thinking Partner**
   - the central reframe
2. **The Executive Model**
   - the control and responsibility lens
3. **Think in Stereo**
   - the core practice
4. **The Empowerment Loop**
   - the repeatable operating system
5. **Forge, Don't Factory**
   - the main craft discipline

Supporting moves:

- `Start with Tension, Not Clarity`
- `Expand, Don't Replace`
- `Turn Curiosity into Output`
- `Develop Taste`
- `Stay in Control`

Conditional / deeper concepts:

- `RBM`
- `MoG`

Current judgment:

The book wins when the first five ideas carry most of the manuscript load.
Everything else should support those ideas rather than compete with them.

---

## 1. AI as Thinking Partner

### Job

Name the central reframe.

### Core Idea

AI is most useful when it functions as a thinking partner in process rather
than as a vending machine for answers.

This moves the reader from:

- output seeking

to:

- co-thinking
- clarification
- testing
- refinement

### Why It Stays

- it is the clearest shift in the book
- it matches the subtitle direction
- it is broad enough to organize multiple domains of life
- it differentiates the book from prompt collections and productivity books

### Risk

It can drift vague unless it is paired with clear ownership language.

That is why it must stay coupled to the Executive Model.

---

## 2. The Executive Model

### Job

Keep judgment, authorship, and responsibility with the human.

### Core Idea

The human is the executive. AI is staff.

The metaphor is not meant to deny collaboration. It is meant to clarify where
final responsibility sits.

This framework answers:

- who decides
- who verifies
- who owns the meaning
- who remains accountable

### Why It Stays

- it resolves the partnership-vs-authority tension
- it gives the book a crisp anti-passivity frame
- it translates well into real behavior instead of remaining abstract

### Risk

It can sound too hierarchical or corporate if used without warmth.

The manuscript should keep pairing it with real co-thinking examples so the
reader feels the collaboration without losing the accountability line.

---

## 3. Think in Stereo

### Job

Teach the core interaction pattern.

### Core Idea

Thinking gets stronger when it moves into dialogue instead of remaining trapped
inside the head.

The reader should:

- speak or write partial thoughts
- react to the AI's response
- sharpen in motion

### Why It Stays

- it is practical immediately
- it fits both personal and professional use
- it connects to Lee's real style of thinking
- it makes the handbook feel active instead of purely conceptual

### Risk

It can collapse into "just talk to the AI more" unless it stays tied to:

- tension
- refinement
- disagreement
- extraction of insight

---

## 4. The Empowerment Loop

### Job

Provide the repeatable operating system of the book.

### Working Loop

1. Notice tension
2. Engage AI
3. Refine
4. Extract insight
5. Apply
6. Repeat and expand

Fuller language such as "think out loud" and "expand self" should stay in the
chapter prose, examples, and exercises rather than making the diagram harder to
reuse.

### Why It Matters

Without this loop, the middle of the book risks becoming a collection of
domain examples:

- work
- creativity
- parenting
- reflection
- identity

With the loop, those chapters become repeated applications of one operating
system.

### Why It Stays

- it is the main connective tissue of the handbook
- it turns the book from philosophy into practice
- it gives the reader a reusable pattern after the book ends

### Risk

It can become too broad if every chapter uses the exact same wording.

The better move is:

- keep the loop explicit
- let each chapter emphasize the stage most relevant to that domain

---

## 5. Forge, Don't Factory

### Job

Name the discipline that keeps the book from collapsing into AI slop.

### Core Idea

One-shot generation is not the point.

The point is pressure, refinement, taste, and formed output.

This framework carries:

- iteration
- disagreement
- quality pressure
- refusal to confuse speed with insight

### Why It Stays

- it protects the whole thesis
- it works in writing, thinking, creation, and decision support
- it gives the reader a memorable anti-slop rule

### Risk

It can become only a slogan if the book does not show the actual pressure moves:

- ask again
- narrow the claim
- contradict the output
- verify
- rewrite

## Supporting Moves

These matter, but they should usually serve the primary stack rather than
compete with it.

### Start with Tension, Not Clarity

**Job:** open the process before perfect understanding exists.

Use it:

- early in the book
- when teaching readers how to begin
- as a recurring permission structure

### Expand, Don't Replace

**Job:** keep the book's moral direction visible.

Use it:

- when the manuscript needs to remind readers what healthy leverage is
- as a contrast frame in the middle and late chapters

### Turn Curiosity into Output

**Job:** connect thought to artifact.

Use it:

- in creativity chapters
- in capability-expansion chapters
- when the manuscript needs momentum

### Develop Taste

**Job:** give the reader a human premium in a world of fluent slop.

Use it:

- in review, selection, and refinement passages
- especially near the risk and authorship chapters

### Stay in Control

**Job:** summarize the accountability side of the book in plain language.

Use it:

- where the reader needs a simple warning or checklist
- in high-stakes chapters and "Do This Now" sections

---

## Conditional / Deeper Concepts

These are strong, but should not carry too much load before the manuscript
earns them.

### RBM

**Job:** name AI-side emergence.

### MoG

**Job:** name human-side leap.

### Why They Stay Deeper For Now

- they are distinctive but not instantly legible
- they work best after the reader already trusts the larger system
- overexposing them too early risks cleverness before usefulness

### Best Use

- Chapter 9
- selected story moments
- appendix or reusable trace/log artifact if that artifact lands well

---

## What To De-Emphasize

- framework proliferation for its own sake
- too many co-equal named concepts in the first half of the book
- any term that sounds clever but does not change reader behavior
- using `RBM` / `MoG` as front-cover positioning before they have earned it
