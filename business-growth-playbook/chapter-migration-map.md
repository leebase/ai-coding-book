# Chapter Migration Map

> How the companion book relates to the structure of *The Growth Consulting Playbook*.

---

## Migration Logic

This book is not a clone of the consulting-leader book.

It uses the same doctrine but changes:

- the protagonist
- the decision surface
- the story emphasis
- the practical moves

Each chapter from the consulting manuscript is sorted into one of three buckets:

- `Transfer`
- `Reframe`
- `Replace`

`Transfer` means the underlying argument largely survives with buyer-side or
enterprise-side examples.

`Reframe` means the core concept survives, but the chapter must be rebuilt
around enterprise leadership decisions.

`Replace` means the consulting-firm chapter logic is too seller-specific and
should be replaced by an enterprise-side equivalent.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Map

## Introduction — The Wrong Win

**Current status:** `Reframe`

What transfers:

- the Efficiency Trap
- the first visible AI win being potentially the wrong win
- the seed-corn doctrine

What changes:

- speak to enterprise leaders, not consulting leaders
- make the felt enemy immediate savings pressure, not delivery-economy pressure
- introduce `capacity` much earlier

Business-leader version:

- open on the temptation to prove AI through cuts
- frame the book as a growth-and-human-amplification playbook for leaders under
  efficiency pressure

## Chapter 1 — The Delivery Economy Is Ending

**Current status:** `Replace`

Why:

- the consulting version is built around software delivery repricing inside a
  consulting business model

Enterprise-side replacement candidate:

- `Chapter 1 — The Efficiency Story Everybody Understands`

Job:

- explain why the reduction-first story feels rational inside enterprises
- show how vendor messaging, CFO pressure, and ROI expectations narrow the
  opening move

## Chapter 2 — The Easy Win

**Current status:** `Transfer`

What transfers:

- why reduction feels rational
- why visible proof outruns strategic judgment
- why the first win is seductive

What changes:

- shift from consultant incentives to enterprise incentive systems
- add the board / budget / operating-pressure layer

## Chapter 3 — What Gets Lost When You Cut Too Deep

**Current status:** `Transfer`

What transfers:

- `Knowledge Carriers`
- invisible losses
- continuity, trust, and judgment

What changes:

- use enterprise examples more than consulting-staffing examples
- expand the Don story as a caution against surface-level performance reads

## Act I Reflection — The Divine Struggle

**Current status:** `Reframe`

What transfers:

- the human stakes are larger than economics

What changes:

- keep the reflection grounded in the book's explicit human stance
- use it to widen the future being argued for, not to drift into memoir

## Chapter 4 — The Multiplier Model

**Current status:** `Reframe`

What transfers:

- substitution road versus empowerment road
- medium-term divergence between the two paths

What changes:

- define the fork for enterprise leaders allocating labor, budget, and new
  capacity
- connect explicitly to `Efficiency Extractor` versus `Capacity Investor`

## Chapter 5 — Who AI Replaces, Who It Multiplies

**Current status:** `Reframe`

What transfers:

- some work compresses
- some people become more valuable
- `Knowledge Carriers` matter more after AI

What changes:

- shift from consulting talent review to enterprise talent and operating
  leverage review
- emphasize who should be amplified, coached, or redeployed

## Chapter 6 — The People You Didn't Know You Had

**Current status:** `Transfer`

What transfers:

- latent capability
- underused people
- amplification as discovery, not just efficiency

What changes:

- make enterprise manager application more direct
- connect the story set to coaching, sponsorship, and tool access

## Chapter 7 — Every Engagement Needs a Growth Thesis

**Current status:** `Reframe`

What transfers:

- the reinvestment question
- the need to decide where the productivity goes

What changes:

- broaden from consulting engagements to transformation efforts, AI programs,
  and capacity-allocation decisions
- likely rename to something closer to `Every AI Program Needs a Growth Thesis`

Anchor story candidate:

- MicroStrategy table-smack story

## Act II Reflection — The Songs

**Current status:** `Transfer`

What transfers:

- human amplification beyond narrow productivity

What changes:

- use as proof that AI can deepen expression and articulation, not only
  enterprise output

## Chapter 8 — Stop Selling the Wrong Work

**Current status:** `Replace`

Why:

- this is a consulting portfolio chapter built from seller logic

Enterprise-side replacement candidate:

- `Chapter 8 — Stop Funding the Wrong Work`

Job:

- help leaders distinguish between AI spend that extracts cost and AI spend that
  expands capability, resilience, or growth

## Chapter 9 — Build the New Service Portfolio

**Current status:** `Replace`

Why:

- the original chapter is about what consulting firms should sell

Enterprise-side replacement candidate:

- `Chapter 9 — Build the Capacity Portfolio`

Job:

  - deeper customer value
  - better internal decision systems
  - new offerings
  - team capability building
  - strategic experiments

## Chapter 10 — Redesign the Engagement

**Current status:** `Reframe`

What transfers:

- redesign of scope, success measures, transfer, and outcomes

What changes:

- move from seller engagement design to buyer-side program and partner design
- show how leaders structure pilots, partner work, governance, and capability

Anchor story candidate:

- Lewis and Clark expedition as bounded first phase with explicit empowerment

## Chapter 11 — Talent and Operating Model

**Current status:** `Reframe`

What transfers:

- apprenticeship
- operating discipline
- who matters more after AI

What changes:

- focus on what type of enterprise operating model can actually compound
  amplified people
- bring in GPLee and AgentFlow-as-personal-COE material as evidence that
  capability systems matter more than isolated experts

## Chapter 12 — The Client Conversation

**Current status:** `Replace`

Why:

- the current chapter is explicitly seller-facing

Enterprise-side replacement candidate:

- `Chapter 12 — The Leadership Conversation`

Job:

- give leaders language for boards, CFOs, peer executives, and partners
- help them defend capacity investment against reduction-only pressure

## Act III Reflection — The Eternal Now

**Current status:** `Transfer`

What transfers:

- the reader is also a person with unreached capacity
- AI can widen intellectual range, not just productive output

What changes:

- make the closing reflection reinforce the book's explicit human future

## Chapter 13 — The New Consulting Firm

**Current status:** `Replace`

Why:

- the synthesis is seller-specific

Enterprise-side replacement candidate:

- `Chapter 13 — The Amplified Enterprise`

Job:

- describe the organization that chooses capacity investment over pure
  extraction
- show what it protects, what it amplifies, and how it grows

## Conclusion — Don't Cut Your Way to the Future

**Current status:** `Reframe`

What transfers:

- central warning
- central invitation

What changes:

- conclude with the human-abundance argument and the leadership choice
- reinforce that a leaner future is not automatically a better one
