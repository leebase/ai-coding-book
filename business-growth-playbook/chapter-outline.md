# Chapter Outline

> Working table of contents and chapter intent notes for *The Business Growth Playbook*.

---

## Introduction — The Wrong Win

**Job:** Open with the central tension. Show why the obvious AI success can be
the beginning of a longer-term failure inside the enterprise.

**Must do:**

- establish the Efficiency Trap for business leaders
- make `Don't Eat Your Seed Corn` feel immediate and memorable
- introduce `capacity` as the real leadership variable
- frame the book as a growth-and-human-amplification playbook, not a tools book
  or a procurement guide

---

## Part I — The Wrong Win

### Chapter 1 — The Efficiency Story Everybody Understands

**Job:** Explain why reduction-first AI strategies feel responsible, legible,
and hard to resist, then show why the early win is real but strategically
incomplete.

**Must do:**

- show why boards, CFOs, and markets reward visible savings first
- explain how vendor narratives reinforce automation and headcount logic
- respect the rationality of the trap before critiquing it
- make the first AI win feel understandable rather than foolish
- show why visible wins outrun invisible future-building
- introduce the language of `capacity misallocation`

### Chapter 2 — What Gets Lost When You Cut Too Deep

**Job:** Make invisible loss visible.

**Must do:**

- introduce `Knowledge Carriers`
- show how cost-cutting can remove context, trust, judgment, and apprenticeship
- expand the Don story into a true seed-corn caution

### Act I Reflection — A Smaller Future Is Not a Better One

**Job:** Widen the stakes beyond the quarter only if the surrounding chapters
cannot carry the human claim cleanly on their own.

**Must do:**

- show why a replace-humans future is not the aspiration
- connect business choices to a larger human future
- introduce a tension or perspective that does not already live inside Chapters
  1 and 2
- keep the moral claim sharp without becoming preachy or merely summary-like

---

## Part II — The Reframe

### Chapter 3 — The Multiplier Model

**Job:** Introduce the central strategic fork for enterprise leaders.

**Must do:**

- contrast the extraction path with the investment path
- connect the fork to `Efficiency Extractor` versus `Capacity Investor`
- show medium-term divergence between the two paths

### Chapter 4 — Who AI Replaces, Who It Multiplies

**Job:** Reframe talent value after AI.

**Must do:**

- show which work compresses
- show which people and capabilities become more valuable
- make the role of amplified humans explicit

### Chapter 5 — The People You Didn't Know You Had

**Job:** Show that AI can surface underused capability inside the organization.

**Must do:**

- move beyond the claim of latent talent and give the reader a real diagnostic
- connect amplification to coaching, sponsorship, and opportunity
- define what signals an underused high-ceiling person:
  - consistently strong judgment
  - trusted context ownership
  - unusual learning velocity
  - broader range than their current box implies
- use lived examples of expanded range rather than generic empowerment talk
- make this a load-bearing proof chapter for the hopeful thesis

### Chapter 6 — Every AI Program Needs a Growth Thesis

**Job:** Force the reader to answer where the capacity goes.

**Must do:**

- ask `What do we do with the capacity AI creates?`
- turn reinvestment from slogan into operating choice
- make the economic case in structured rather than spreadsheet terms:
  what leaders give up in immediate savings and what they gain in capability,
  resilience, optionality, and growth over time
- force three questions every time `capacity` appears:
  - capacity for what
  - capacity in whom
  - capacity measured how
- use the MicroStrategy story or equivalent proof that business value must be
  defined before automation is scoped

### Act II Reflection — What Amplified Humans Can Become

**Job:** Give the reader a brighter picture of human possibility only if it adds
something Chapters 4-7 cannot carry themselves.

**Must do:**

- show that amplification applies to expression, thought, and initiative, not
  only to throughput
- introduce a perspective that does not belong naturally inside the adjacent
  chapters
- widen the reader's imagination without losing business seriousness

---

## Part III — The Capacity Playbook

### Chapter 7 — Stop Funding the Wrong Work

**Job:** Help leaders distinguish between AI spend that extracts cost and AI
spend that builds the future.

**Must do:**

- identify the patterns of AI investment that produce theater instead of
  capability
- show what should lose funding priority when growth is the real goal
- make the reader more dangerous in portfolio conversations

### Chapter 8 — Build the Capacity Portfolio

**Job:** Show where AI-created capacity can be invested.

**Must do:**

- define the main destination categories for reinvested capacity
- connect capacity investment to growth, resilience, capability, and new value
- keep the guidance concrete enough for Monday-morning decisions

### Chapter 9 — Redesign the Program

**Job:** Show how leaders structure pilots, partner work, and governance for
amplification rather than mere extraction.

**Must do:**

- use Lewis and Clark as the bounded first-phase model
- make Lewis and Clark operational rather than romantic:
  - who gets chosen
  - what protection they get
  - what mission they are sent on
  - how learning transfers back
  - why this beats committee-scale rollout
- show how to structure a serious expedition without requiring enterprise-wide
  rollout
- explain what leaders should measure, protect, and transfer

### Chapter 10 — Talent and Operating Model

**Job:** Describe the kind of enterprise system that can actually compound
amplified people.

**Must do:**

- address coaching, apprenticeship, and operating discipline
- keep the main focus on how amplified people are coached, developed, and
  sustained inside an enterprise
- use GPLee as the cultural proof point
- use methodology / COE material only insofar as it proves that systems beat
  heroics and may fit better here than in Chapter 10
- make the case that systems beat isolated heroics

### Chapter 11 — The Leadership Conversation

**Job:** Give the reader language for boards, CFOs, peer executives, and
partners.

**Must do:**

- help leaders defend capacity investment against savings-only logic
- clarify how to explain the twin mandate of efficiency plus empowerment
- show how to work with partners without surrendering the leadership goal
- include an explicit buyer's framework for outside partners:
  the questions that reveal whether a partner will help invest capacity or
  merely harvest savings

---

## Part IV — The Future You Choose

### Chapter 12 — The Amplified Enterprise

**Job:** Synthesize what an organization looks like when it chooses amplified
humans over simple extraction.

**Must do:**

- describe the future organization in practical terms
- elevate small trusted teams with disproportionate reach
- use the Lewis and Clark pattern as organization design, not just as a story

---

## Conclusion — Don't Cut Your Way to the Future

**Job:** Close on the warning and the invitation.

**Must do:**

- restate the trap
- restate the brighter future
- watch overlap with Chapter 13 and collapse duplicated future-state work if one
  section starts absorbing the other
- leave the reader with a choice about what kind of leader they will be when
  given leverage

---

## First Chapter Recommendation

**Draft first:** `Introduction — The Wrong Win`

### Why

- it forces the doctrine to work in business-leader language
- it reveals quickly whether the book sounds human enough and sharp enough
- it will become reusable proposal and talk material
- it establishes whether `capacity`, `seed corn`, and amplified humans can all
  coexist cleanly from page one
