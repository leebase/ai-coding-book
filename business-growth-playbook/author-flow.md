# AuthorFlow

> Running methodology log for how *The Business Growth Playbook* is being developed. Keep this file current so the eventual writing about the process is grounded in what actually happened.

---

## Purpose

This file tracks:

- how the companion book is being framed
- what changed from the consulting-leader book and why
- which stories, frameworks, and audience decisions are proving strongest
- what the paired-book method appears to be becoming

---

## Working Definition

This companion book is being built with the same AuthorFlow discipline used for
*The Growth Consulting Playbook*:

1. start with thesis, not prose
2. mine lived stories before abstracting them into frameworks
3. pressure-test the book through audience reality, not only conceptual elegance
4. keep the process visible enough to be written about later

The difference is that this book begins with an existing intellectual sibling.
That creates a new method question:

How do you build a companion book that shares a doctrine without becoming a
repackaged edition?

Current answer:

- same prescription
- different protagonist
- different decisions
- different story emphasis
- different practical playbook

---

## Core Companion-Book Rules

- Preserve the shared doctrine with the consulting book.
- Do not let the new book collapse into "how to buy better consulting."
- Keep consultants visible, but as partners in a larger enterprise mandate.
- Treat `Don't Eat Your Seed Corn` as a governing idea, not a colorful phrase.
- Elevate `capacity` as the book's main unit of analysis.
- Keep the human thesis explicit: AI does not create abundance by itself.
  Empowered humans do.

## Running Log

## 2026-03-28 — Companion Book Direction Locked

The project shifted from "audience-flipped edition" thinking to a clearer
paired-book concept:

- Book A: *The Growth Consulting Playbook*
- Book B: *The Business Growth Playbook*

Why it matters:

- The two books now form a two-sided market narrative rather than a primary
  book and a watered-down adaptation.
- The intellectual family is preserved without forcing the second book to reuse
  the first book's protagonist or promise.

Method signal:

- AuthorFlow can support paired books when the shared doctrine is locked before
  chapter planning begins.

## 2026-03-28 — "Buy Better Consulting" Rejected as the Headline

An early framing risk was identified and explicitly rejected.

The business-leader book is not primarily about buying better consultants. It is
about what leaders do with AI-created capacity.

Why it matters:

- It preserves agency inside the enterprise.
- It keeps the book from collapsing into procurement logic.
- It makes consulting one execution variable inside a broader leadership thesis.

Method signal:

- When building a companion book, define what stays supporting machinery and
  what becomes the main subject.

## 2026-03-28 — Human Abundance Locked as the Moral Center

The thesis sharpened from reinvestment language alone into a stronger human
claim:

- AI does not create abundance.
- Abundance comes from empowered humans.

Why it matters:

- It turns the book from a narrow strategy memo into a real point of view about
  the future of organizations and society.
- It clarifies that the book is arguing against a replace-humans future, not
  merely against premature layoffs.

Method signal:

- The moral center of a nonfiction book should be named explicitly before
  chapter architecture hardens around a weaker version.

## 2026-03-28 — Capacity Became the Control Variable

The central operating question is now:

`What do we do with the capacity AI creates?`

Why it matters:

- `capacity` is more usable than generic productivity talk
- it forces allocation decisions
- it links efficiency gains to future growth, capability, and organizational
  design

Method signal:

- When a book's thesis is partly economic, find the controllable variable early.

## 2026-03-28 — Seed Corn Promoted from Metaphor to Doctrine

`Don't Eat Your Seed Corn` is no longer supporting rhetoric. It is now a core
thesis thread.

Why it matters:

- It makes irreversibility visible.
- It adds consequence to the efficiency argument.
- It gives readers a memorable test for whether a decision improves the quarter
  by weakening the future.

Method signal:

- Founder language often contains the doctrine before the formal framework
  exists. Preserve it, then promote it deliberately when it proves durable.

## 2026-03-28 — Leadership Identity Shift Named

The book now uses a direct identity mirror:

- `Efficiency Extractor`
- `Capacity Investor`

Why it matters:

- It gives the reader a diagnosis, not just a framework.
- It turns the book into a judgment about leadership behavior under leverage.

Method signal:

- Identity language can make a business book more teachable than abstract
  strategy language alone.

## 2026-03-28 — Lewis and Clark Elevated from Anecdote to Organizational Model

The Lewis and Clark proposal is now a primary recurring anchor for the book,
especially the line:

`Give me five high performers and I will coach them in the use of AI.`

Why it matters:

- The story proves that amplified humans, not generalized tooling, are the real
  source of abundance.
- It models the future organization as small, trusted, high-judgment teams with
  disproportionate reach.

Method signal:

- When a story explains both the thesis and the operating model, promote it from
  illustration to structural anchor.

## 2026-03-28 — Story Mining Scope Expanded Beyond the Consulting Manuscript

This book will draw from Lee's broader story set, not just stories already
deployed in the consulting-leader book.

Priority story patterns:

- amplified experts
- protected Knowledge Carriers
- coached latent performers
- false savings and invisible losses
- small teams with outsized reach
- human flourishing beyond enterprise productivity

Method signal:

- Companion books need shared doctrine but not a recycled proof set.

## 2026-03-28 — Enterprise-Side Review Model Added

The shared reviewer set was extended for this book with three new reviewer
agents:

- `enterprise-transformation-reviewer`
- `business-unit-leader-reviewer`
- `cfo-reviewer`

Why it matters:

- The consulting reviewers from the first book do not fully pressure-test the
  enterprise-side realities of this companion.
- This book needs explicit scrutiny from the point of view of operators under
  governance pressure, leaders responsible for growth, and executives who
  measure success through financial tension.

Method signal:

- Paired books may share a doctrine while needing different review lenses.
- Add new reviewers before drafting if the protagonist, incentives, and failure
  modes materially change.

## 2026-03-28 — Companion Workspace Promoted from Concept Package to Book Scaffold

The project moved from a concept-only package into a real standalone book

Artifacts added:

## 2026-03-28 — "Handbook" Became a Drafting Constraint, Not Just a Positioning Word

Lee clarified that this book should read as a handbook.

Why it matters:

- The manuscript cannot stop at elegant doctrine and strong contrasts.
- Each chapter now has to produce a usable management move, diagnostic, or
  operating question that a leader could carry into a real staffing, funding,
  or program conversation.
- This especially affects the middle chapters, where the risk is drifting into
  persuasive nonfiction when the promise is really a business handbook.

Method signal:

- When the human names the form more precisely, treat it as a drafting
  constraint immediately.
- In practice, that means turning framework chapters into chapters with
  reusable tests, Monday-morning moves, and decision lenses.

## 2026-03-28 — Research Added as Reinforcement, Not as Driver

Lee asked for research support to be added carefully:

- not enough to turn the book into a study review
- enough to add authority and texture
- with exact sourcing and no hallucinated references

Artifacts added:

- `research-support.md`
- `reference-ledger.md`

Why it matters:

- This creates a clean separation between doctrine, lived proof, and external
  support.
- It gives the manuscript a way to use research as spice instead of as the main
  ingredient.
- It lowers the risk of fuzzy-memory citations, which is especially important
  in a book making contrarian claims to executives.

Method signal:

- In an AuthorFlow project, research support should be curated to the thesis,
  not accumulated for its own sake.
- A reference ledger is worth maintaining when the author wants authority
  without drift into literature-summary mode.

## 2026-03-28 — Early Chapters Received a Light Research Layer

The introduction and Chapters 1-4 were updated with short, verified research
references.

What changed in practice:

- adoption-speed context was added early
- augmentation studies were used to support heterogeneous gains rather than
  simplistic replacement claims
- tacit-knowledge and organizational-forgetting research was used to support
  the invisible-loss argument
- incentive-bias research was used to reinforce the pressure thesis

Why it matters:

- The manuscript now has more authority without becoming less personal.
- The experience of the chapter did not change from "handbook" to "study
  review," which was the core risk Lee wanted to avoid.

Method signal:

- The right amount of research in a thought-leadership handbook is often one
  well-placed paragraph, not a page of proof.

## 2026-03-28 — Research Trimmed Back Toward Voice

After the first insertion pass, the next judgment call was not "add more" but
"keep control of the tone."

What changed:

- named scholars were removed from most in-chapter mentions
- research references were kept where they sharpened authority
- citations were translated back into Lee's operational meaning immediately

Why it matters:

- the manuscript's authority comes primarily from lived pattern recognition and
  trusted-advisor clarity
- research works best here when it nods at the pattern instead of speaking for
  it

Method signal:

- in a handbook like this, research should validate the lived pattern without
  becoming the dominant voice on the page

## 2026-03-28 — Chapter 5 Locked the "Handbook of Human Amplification" Direction

Chapter 5 forced an important choice:

Would the hopeful side of the book remain inspirational language, or would it
become operational?

The chapter was written as a practical talent-diagnostic chapter instead of a
general essay about hidden potential.

What that required:

- defining repeatable signals of an underused high-ceiling person
- adding a simple multiplier-candidate test
- ending with a Monday-morning talent scan
- keeping the chapter disciplined against romanticizing everyone as hidden
  greatness

Why it matters:

- The book's human stance is now more credible because it produces management
  behavior, not just aspiration.
- "Amplify people" is starting to mean something operational on the page.

Method signal:

- A thought-leadership handbook becomes real when its hopeful chapters can be
  used, not just admired.

## 2026-03-28 — Chapter 6 Forced the Book to Make an Economic Argument

Chapter 6 was the point where the book could no longer rely on diagnosis,
identity shift, or talent language alone.

It had to answer:

- where the capacity goes
- how a leader should think about harvest versus reinvestment
- what makes a growth thesis more than a slogan

What worked:

- treating the chapter as an allocation chapter, not an inspiration chapter
- using the MicroStrategy story shape to force business value to come before
  technical motion
- adding a simple harvest / strengthen / build pattern instead of fake
  precision

Why it matters:

- This is the chapter where the book starts earning the right to challenge a
  CFO instinct rather than only naming it.
- The manuscript is now moving from "who to bet on" to "what those bets are
  for."

Method signal:

- When a handbook reaches its economic center, structured tradeoffs matter more
  than elegant slogans.

## 2026-03-28 — Chapter 6 Tightened from Review Feedback

The first draft of Chapter 6 worked, but the review feedback pointed to a final
important refinement:

- make the growth-thesis definition sharper
- give the four-part framework slightly more operating weight
- keep one bridge back to the human-amplification logic so the chapter does not
  feel detached from Chapters 4 and 5

Why it matters:

- This keeps the chapter from becoming a smart abstract allocation essay.
- It preserves the book's pattern of translating doctrine into deployable
  leadership tools.

Method signal:

- When a core chapter is already strong, refinement should increase usability
  rather than add new conceptual mass.

## 2026-03-28 — Chapter 7 Turned the Growth Thesis into a Portfolio Filter

Chapter 7 had one main job:

stop the manuscript from staying at the level of good intentions.

What the chapter needed to do:

- identify the kinds of AI work that look modern but do not build the future
- give leaders a way to sort useful work from strategic work
- turn the growth thesis from a statement into a funding filter

What worked:

- naming AI theater as real activity with weak future-building value
- using a three-level portfolio filter instead of a long taxonomy
- ending with a one-page-style portfolio review

Why it matters:

- The manuscript is now teaching leaders how to stop financing the wrong work,
  not just how to admire the right language.

Method signal:

- Portfolio chapters should help the reader stop doing something, not only
  start doing something.

## 2026-03-28 — Chapter 8 Completed the Positive Side of the Portfolio Logic

After Chapter 7's negative filter, Chapter 8 had to answer a different
question:

once leaders stop funding the wrong work, where should the living capacity
actually go?

What worked:

- giving the portfolio four clear destination categories
- keeping Chapter 8 distinct from Chapter 6 by focusing on categories and
  choices, not on the thesis argument again
- tying the earlier "range" idea to portfolio decisions
- ending with a top-three-bets exercise so the chapter stays handbook-like

Why it matters:

- Chapters 6-8 now act more like an executive operating system than three
  adjacent idea chapters.

Method signal:

- When adjacent chapters risk collapse into each other, give each one a
  different job:
  thesis, filter, destination.

## 2026-03-28 — Chapter 9 Moved the Book from Allocation into Program Design

Chapter 9 had to do something the earlier playbook chapters did not:

turn a strategic argument into a first-phase execution design.

What worked:

- treating Lewis and Clark as an operating model instead of a metaphor
- making the chapter answer six concrete design questions
- framing protection and coaching as part of the experimental design rather
  than as nice-to-have support
- keeping the contrast with committee-scale rollout very explicit

Why it matters:

- This is one of the most proprietary chapters in the manuscript.
- It is where the book starts to tell leaders not only what to believe and fund
  but how to structure the first serious move.

Method signal:

- Distinctive stories become most valuable when they are translated into design
  decisions the reader can actually run.

## 2026-03-28 — Chapter 10 Turned Expeditions into a Capability System

Chapter 10 had a different job from Chapter 9.

Chapter 9 answered:

- how to run the first serious expedition

Chapter 10 had to answer:

- how the organization stops depending on one successful expedition or a few
  heroes

What worked:

- framing heroics as proof rather than as solution
- making coaching, apprenticeship, and transfer part of the operating model
- connecting Lewis and Clark to COE logic
- using AgentFlow as a personal-scale analogy for how systems preserve learning

Why it matters:

- This chapter makes the manuscript's "operating system" claim much more
  believable.
- It explains how amplified capability compounds rather than resetting each
  time the organization starts a new wave.

Method signal:

- Execution chapters should be followed by compounding-system chapters, not by
  more isolated examples.

## 2026-03-28 — GPLee Reframed as Cultural Spread, Not Insider Label

Lee clarified an important factual and rhetorical point:

- `GPLee` was an internal nickname from being an early adopter and influencer
  around GenAI
- the important proof was not the nickname itself
- the important proof was that direct reports later produced exceptional
  self-reviews and explicitly said they had used ChatGPT the way Lee had taught
  them

Why it matters:

- external readers need the pattern, not the insider shorthand
- this turns the story into proof that coached capability can spread culturally
  through a team

Method signal:

- when a personal label appears in a business book, translate it into the
  pattern it proves unless the label itself carries broad reader meaning.
- This creates a reusable tutorial trail for how AuthorFlow moves from concept
  package into drafting readiness.

Method signal:

- Once the doctrine is stable, create local continuity files before chapter
  drafting begins.
- The transition from "book concept" to "book scaffold" is a distinct phase
  worth logging explicitly.

External feedback converged on the same warning:

Why it matters:

Method signal:

- This stage is likely reusable in future tutorials because it converts

## 2026-03-28 — Credibility Strategy Made Explicit

Before the rest of the drafting begins, the project locked a separate
credibility strategy artifact:

- `credibility-strategy.md`

Why it matters:

- This book makes a contrarian claim to enterprise leaders but is not written
  by a Fortune 100 CEO or a CFO.
- The manuscript therefore needs a deliberate authority posture.

The chosen posture is:

- pattern authority, not positional authority
- trusted-advisor authority, not borrowed executive persona
- witnessed consequence, not prestige signaling
- tradeoff fluency, not anti-efficiency rhetoric

The introduction was then updated to make that stance explicit once, early,
without over-explaining it.

Method signal:

- When the author's vantage point differs from the reader's formal role, it is
  worth locking the credibility strategy as its own artifact before too much
  prose accumulates.
