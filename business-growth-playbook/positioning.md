# Positioning

> Positioning notes for *The Business Growth Playbook*.

---

## Title

**The Business Growth Playbook: How Leaders Use AI to Amplify People and Grow the Business**

---

## Category

This is a strategy and leadership book for enterprise leaders operating in the
AI era.

It is not:

- a prompt book
- a tools guide
- a procurement handbook
- a generic future-of-work book

It sits at the intersection of:

- AI strategy
- transformation leadership
- operating model design
- human capability and growth

---

## Core Positioning

Most AI leadership narratives still equate success with automation, efficiency,
and cost reduction.

This book argues that those wins are often strategically incomplete.

AI creates surplus capacity. The real leadership question is what happens to
that capacity:

- Is it harvested as cost savings?
- Or is it invested in people, capability, judgment, and growth?

That is the center of the book.

---

## Pairing With the Consulting Book

This book is designed to pair directly with *The Growth Consulting Playbook*.

The relationship is clear:

- consulting book: how consulting leaders should sell and deliver the right
  kind of AI work
- business book: how enterprise leaders should use AI-created capacity to
  amplify people and grow the business

Same prescription, different lens.

This matters because the two books can create a shared language across buyer and
seller rather than leaving each side trapped in a different mental model.

---

## Why This Book Matters Now

- enterprises are under pressure to show immediate AI ROI
- vendor narratives keep reinforcing automation and headcount reduction as the
  default proof of value
- leaders can now generate real capacity quickly, but many still lack a
  framework for allocating it well
- the market is early enough that a strong leadership doctrine can still shape
  behavior rather than merely comment on it

This is not a future-tense book. It is a now-book for leaders already making
these tradeoffs.

---

## What Makes It Different

### 1. It is not AI triumphalism

The book does not claim AI creates abundance by itself.

It claims abundance comes from empowered humans.

### 2. It names the invisible failure

Most books celebrate visible efficiency wins.

This book names `capacity misallocation`: the business saves money, but no one
notices what was never built.

### 3. It treats the leadership choice as moral and strategic

The question is not only "what works?"

It is also:

What kind of organization are you building when given leverage?

### 4. It pairs with a seller-side companion

Few books define a common doctrine across both sides of the consulting and
enterprise relationship.

This one can.

---

## Core Promise

This book will help leaders:

- recognize the Efficiency Trap
- avoid eating their seed corn
- decide where AI-created capacity should go
- identify which people should be amplified rather than reduced
- defend capability investment against reduction-only pressure
- build a brighter future for the organization, not just a leaner one

---

## Back-Cover Direction

AI is already helping organizations do more work with fewer people. That makes
cost reduction look like the responsible next move. But leaders who use AI
mainly to cut cost may be improving the quarter while weakening the future.
They can remove context, judgment, and the very people who could have turned new
capacity into new growth.

*The Business Growth Playbook* argues that AI does not create abundance by
itself. Empowered humans do. Lee Harrington shows leaders how to use AI-created
capacity to amplify people, protect what matters, and grow the business rather
than simply shrinking it. The result is a playbook for leaders who want a
better future for their organizations than efficiency alone can buy.

---

## Positioning Risks

- `human abundance` language can drift too soft if not kept close to
  consequence, capacity, and seed corn
- consultant references can become too prominent and confuse the real subject
- the book can sound merely inspirational unless each major concept maps to a
  concrete leadership decision

---

## Short Pitch

AI makes it easier to create surplus capacity. The leaders who win will not be
the ones who only use that capacity to cut cost. They will be the ones who use
it to amplify people, protect future capability, and grow the business.
