# Proof Map

> Story-to-doctrine and chapter proof obligations for the manuscript.

---

## Purpose

This file exists to prevent the manuscript from settling for elegant slogans.

Each major chapter should prove something specific:

- what the reader should now believe
- which doctrine it advances
- which story or case shape carries the proof

## Chapter Proof Obligations

## Introduction — The Wrong Win

- Reader should believe:
  the obvious AI success can be the beginning of a longer-term failure.
- Must prove:
  efficiency alone is too small a definition of success.
- Best proof shape:
  doctrine stack plus a vivid opening example or compressed story logic.

## Chapter 1 — The Efficiency Story Everybody Understands

- Reader should believe:
  reduction-first logic is rational, but not sufficient.
- Must prove:
  the trap comes from pressure, incentives, and legible ROI, not from shallow
  thinking alone.
- Best proof shape:
  enterprise pressure logic plus one visible-win / incomplete-win pattern.

## Chapter 2 — What Gets Lost When You Cut Too Deep

- Reader should believe:
  organizations destroy more than salary cost when they cut blindly.
- Must prove:
  invisible assets are real and strategically material.
- Best proof shape:
  Saving Don / Knowledge Carrier forensics.

## Chapter 3 — The Multiplier Model

- Reader should believe:
  AI creates a real fork in leadership behavior.
- Must prove:
  extraction and investment lead to materially different futures.
- Best proof shape:
  Rach 2 Day plus a medium-term comparison of the two paths.

## Chapter 4 — Who AI Replaces, Who It Multiplies

- Reader should believe:
  some work compresses, but some people become more valuable.
- Must prove:
  amplified humans are not a sentimental exception but a strategic category.
- Best proof shape:
  Knowledge Carrier logic plus enterprise examples of leverage.

## Chapter 5 — The People You Didn't Know You Had

- Reader should believe:
  latent high-ceiling people already exist inside many organizations.
- Must prove:
  leaders can identify and amplify them intentionally.
- Best proof shape:
  coached-range-expansion stories and a practical diagnostic.

## Chapter 6 — Every AI Program Needs a Growth Thesis

- Reader should believe:
  capacity must be allocated, not merely celebrated.
- Must prove:
  reinvestment has an economic and strategic return profile that pure
  extraction does not.
- Best proof shape:
  MicroStrategy table smack plus a structured reinvestment argument.

## Chapter 7 — Stop Funding the Wrong Work

- Reader should believe:
  some AI investments produce theater rather than future-building.
- Must prove:
  portfolio decisions can either amplify or hollow out the enterprise.
- Best proof shape:
  concrete categories of work to stop funding or lower in priority.

## Chapter 8 — Build the Capacity Portfolio

- Reader should believe:
  there are concrete destinations for reinvested capacity.
- Must prove:
  leaders can choose among distinct types of future-building investment.
- Best proof shape:
  destination categories with examples and tradeoffs.

## Chapter 9 — Redesign the Program

- Reader should believe:
  bounded, trusted, high-leverage expeditions outperform diffuse rollout
  theater.
- Must prove:
  Lewis and Clark is an operating model, not just a metaphor.
- Best proof shape:
  five-high-performers pattern with explicit mission, protections, and learning
  transfer.

## Chapter 10 — Talent and Operating Model

- Reader should believe:
  amplified people do not compound by magic; they require systems.
- Must prove:
  coaching, apprenticeship, and operating discipline are part of the economic
  case, not a soft add-on.
- Best proof shape:
  GPLee plus capability-system examples.

## Chapter 11 — The Leadership Conversation

- Reader should believe:
  leaders must define the mandate before partners, peers, or finance functions
  define it for them.
- Must prove:
  there is a usable language for defending capacity investment and selecting the
  right outside help.
- Best proof shape:
  board/CFO/partner language plus a simple partner-evaluation framework.

## Chapter 12 — The Amplified Enterprise

- Reader should believe:
  a stronger organizational future is both imaginable and practical.
- Must prove:
  small trusted teams, protected context, and amplified people can become a
  real organizational model.
- Best proof shape:
  Lewis and Clark elevated to organization design plus supporting operating
  patterns.

---

## Drafting Rule

If a chapter cannot answer:

- what the reader now believes
- what doctrine it proved
- what concrete story or case shape carried that proof

then the chapter is not ready to draft.
