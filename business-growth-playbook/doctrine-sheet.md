# Doctrine Sheet

> Working definitions for the book's key terms. Use this to keep the manuscript
> sharp and consistent.

---

## Core Rule

The manuscript should earn the doctrine, not keep inventing it.

Each major term below should mean one clear thing and should appear with proof,
not only with rhetoric.

---

## 1. The Efficiency Trap

### Definition

The Efficiency Trap is the mistake of treating visible AI-driven savings as the
full measure of success, even when those savings weaken judgment, continuity,
capability, and future growth.

### What It Is Not

- a claim that efficiency is bad
- a claim that no automation should happen
- a claim that every cost reduction is destructive

### Proof Obligation

Show at least one case where the early win looked rational and even successful,
but thinned the business strategically.

---

## 2. Don't Eat Your Seed Corn

### Definition

`Don't Eat Your Seed Corn` is the doctrine that leaders can improve the present
by consuming the people, context, and optionality that should have funded the
future.

### What It Is Not

- a generic warning against layoffs
- a sentimental defense of every existing role

### Proof Obligation

Show a decision where visible savings came out of tomorrow, not only today.

---

## 3. Capacity

### Definition

`Capacity` is the usable strategic surplus AI creates: time, range, judgment
surface, throughput, and initiative that can now be directed somewhere.

### Manuscript Rule

Every time the manuscript uses `capacity`, force one or more of these
follow-up questions:

1. capacity for what
2. capacity in whom
3. capacity measured how

### Proof Obligation

The book should repeatedly show where capacity actually goes, not merely claim
that it exists.

---

## 4. Capacity Misallocation

### Definition

`Capacity misallocation` is the invisible failure where AI-created capacity is
harvested for visible savings, but no one notices what was never built,
developed, or attempted.

### What It Makes Visible

- invisible losses
- failed reinvestment
- underbuilt futures

### Proof Obligation

Name at least one case where a strategy looked successful in near-term metrics
while still weakening the future of the business.

---

## 5. Knowledge Carriers

### Definition

`Knowledge Carriers` are the people whose value is larger than their visible
output because they hold context, trust, judgment, relationship continuity, and
cross-functional coherence the organization cannot easily reconstruct.

### What It Is Not

- only the oldest or most senior people
- only technical experts

### Proof Obligation

Use a person-shaped story, not merely an abstract warning.

---

## 6. Efficiency Extractor

### Definition

An `Efficiency Extractor` is a leader who treats AI-created leverage primarily
as a way to capture cost savings and optimize the current system.

### Behavior Markers

- savings first
- visible proof first
- people treated mainly as cost surface
- future-building subordinated to immediate ROI

---

## 7. Capacity Investor

### Definition

A `Capacity Investor` is a leader who treats AI-created capacity as an asset to
allocate into people, capability, resilience, and growth while still respecting
the importance of efficiency.

### Behavior Markers

- reinvestment choices made explicitly
- amplified people chosen intentionally
- capability and context protected
- future options expanded, not only current cost reduced

### Proof Obligation

Show at least one decision point where a leader had to choose where capacity
would actually go.

---

## 8. The Lewis and Clark Pattern

### Definition

The Lewis and Clark Pattern is a bounded, high-trust, empowerment-first model
for enterprise AI adoption:

- choose exceptional people
- give them real leverage
- coach them seriously
- protect the mission
- learn before scaling

### What It Is Not

- romantic pilot language
- innovation theater
- vague “start small” advice

### Proof Obligation

Cash it out operationally:

- who gets chosen
- what protections they receive
- what mission they are given
- how learning transfers back
- why this beats committee-scale rollout

---

## 9. Human Abundance

### Definition

`Human abundance` is the future condition in which AI allows people and small
teams to operate with more reach, judgment, expression, and contribution than
old organizational models expected.

### Usage Rule

Prefer the harder vocabulary in the body of the manuscript:

- seed corn
- capacity
- Knowledge Carriers
- Capacity Investor

Let `human abundance` function more as banner language and conclusion logic than
as the manuscript's everyday operating term.
