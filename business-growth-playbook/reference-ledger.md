## 1. Generative AI at Work

- Source:
  Erik Brynjolfsson, Danielle Li, and Lindsey R. Raymond, *Generative AI at
  Work*, NBER Working Paper 31161, 2023.
- URL:
  https://www.nber.org/papers/w31161
- Exact support checked:
  - average productivity increase of 14%
  - 34% improvement for novice and low-skilled workers
  - minimal impact on experienced and highly skilled workers
  - suggestive evidence that the AI disseminates best practices of more able
    workers
- Evidence type:
  empirical field study
- Best chapters:
  Introduction, 3, 4, 10
- Manuscript-safe use:
  paraphrase the heterogeneous productivity and learning pattern
- Status:
  verified

## 2. Navigating the Jagged Technological Frontier

- Source:
  Fabrizio Dell'Acqua, Saran Rajendran, Edward McFowland III, Lisa Krayer,
  Ethan Mollick, François Candelon, Hila Lifshitz-Assaf, Karim R. Lakhani, and
  Katherine C. Kellogg, *Navigating the Jagged Technological Frontier: Field
  Experimental Evidence of the Effects of AI on Knowledge Worker Productivity
  and Quality*, HBS Working Paper 24-013.
- URL:
  https://www.hbs.edu/ris/Publication%20Files/24-013_d9b45b68-9e74-42d6-a1c6-c72fb70c7282.pdf
- Exact support checked:
  - consultants using AI on tasks inside the frontier completed 12.2% more
    tasks on average
  - completed tasks 25.1% more quickly
  - produced more than 40% higher quality results than the control group
  - below-average performers improved by 43%; above-average performers improved
    by 17%
  - for tasks outside the frontier, performance worsened
- Evidence type:
  randomized field experiment
- Best chapters:
  3, 4, 9
- Manuscript-safe use:
  paraphrase the unevenness of augmentation and the risk of overtrust outside
  the frontier
- Status:
  verified

## 3. Building Pro-Worker Artificial Intelligence

- Source:
  Daron Acemoglu, David Autor, and Simon Johnson, *Building Pro-Worker
  Artificial Intelligence*, NBER Working Paper 34854, 2026.
- URL:
  https://www.nber.org/papers/w34854
- Exact support checked:
  - AI can extend human judgment, enable new tasks, and accelerate skill
    acquisition
  - this potential is currently underexploited
  - misaligned incentives, path dependence, and a pro-automation ideology may
    lead to underinvestment in pro-worker AI
- Evidence type:
  analytical / normative working paper
- Best chapters:
  Introduction, 1, 3, 6, 11
- Manuscript-safe use:
  use as a framing source for incentive bias and augmentation potential, not as
  direct causal proof
- Status:
  verified

## 4. The Effects of Generative AI on High-Skilled Work

- Source:
  Zubin Cui, Mehtabul Azam Demirer, Sonia Jaffe, Lukas Musolff, Sida Peng, and
  Tobias Salz, *The Effects of Generative AI on High-Skilled Work: Evidence
  from Three Field Experiments with Software Developers*, working paper draft,
  2026.
- URL:
  https://economics.mit.edu/sites/default/files/inline-files/draft_copilot_experiments.pdf
- Exact support checked:
  - three randomized experiments across Microsoft, Accenture, and one anonymous
    company
  - 4,867 developers in combined analysis
  - 26.08% increase in completed tasks among developers using the tool
  - less experienced developers had higher adoption rates and greater
    productivity gains
- Evidence type:
  randomized field experiments
- Best chapters:
  3, 4, 10
- Manuscript-safe use:
  use as support that AI can materially amplify high-skilled work and that
  effects are heterogeneous
- Status:
  verified

## 5. Commercializing Knowledge: University Science, Knowledge Capture, and Firm Performance in Biotechnology

- Source:
  Lynne G. Zucker, Michael R. Darby, and Jeff S. Armstrong, *Commercializing
  Knowledge: University Science, Knowledge Capture, and Firm Performance in
  Biotechnology*, NBER Working Paper 8499, 2001; published in *Management
  Science*, 2002.
- URL:
  https://www.nber.org/papers/w8499
- Exact support checked:
  - tacit, complex discoveries are not cleanly captured through codified
    formulas alone
  - team production allows more tacit knowledge capture
  - tacit knowledge capture strongly predicted firm success in the study
- Evidence type:
  empirical / econometric study
- Best chapters:
  2, 4, 10
- Manuscript-safe use:
  support the importance of tacit knowledge and hard-to-codify expertise
- Status:
  verified

## 6. Does Organizational Forgetting Affect Vendor Quality Performance?

- Source:
  A. Agrawal and S. Muthulingam, *Does Organizational Forgetting Affect Vendor
  Quality Performance? An Empirical Investigation*, *Manufacturing & Service
  Operations Management*, 17(3), 2015.
- URL:
  https://doi.org/10.1287/msom.2015.0522
- Summary page inspected:
  https://sites.psu.edu/cscrresearchportal/2015/04/01/does-organizational-forgetting-affect-vendor-quality-performance-an-empirical-investigation/
- Exact support checked:
  - more than 16% of quality gains from autonomous learning and 13% from
    induced learning depreciated every year
  - forgetting was lower when knowledge was embedded in technology than when it
    was embedded in routines or organizational members
- Evidence type:
  empirical operations-management study
- Best chapters:
  2, 4, 10
- Manuscript-safe use:
  support the claim that organizational forgetting is measurable and costly
- Status:
  use with care

Note:
We inspected a credible summary page with DOI and journal details, not the full
publisher PDF. Fine for internal guidance, but if we quote this closely in the
manuscript we should inspect the journal version directly first.

## 7. The Rapid Adoption of Generative AI

- Source:
  Alexander Bick, Adam Blandin, and David J. Deming, *The Rapid Adoption of
  Generative AI*, NBER Working Paper 32966, 2024; revised 2025.
- URL:
  https://www.nber.org/papers/w32966
- Exact support checked:
  - as of late 2024, nearly 40% of the U.S. population age 18-64 used
    generative AI
  - 23% of employed respondents had used generative AI for work at least once
    in the previous week
  - 9% used it every work day
  - between 1% and 5% of all work hours were currently assisted by generative
    AI
  - reported time savings were equivalent to 1.4% of total work hours
- Evidence type:
  nationally representative survey / working paper
- Best chapters:
  Introduction, 1, 6, Conclusion
- Manuscript-safe use:
  use for adoption speed and early time-savings context, not for strong
  long-run claims
- Status:
  verified

## 8. AI's Use of Knowledge in Society

- Source:
  Erik Brynjolfsson and Zoë Hitzig, *AI's Use of Knowledge in Society*, chapter
  in *The Economics of Transformative AI*, 2025.
- URL:
  https://www.nber.org/books-and-chapters/economics-transformative-ai/ais-use-knowledge-society
- Exact support checked:
  - AI can codify some previously tacit and inalienable local knowledge
  - AI can shift the locus of control by changing who can aggregate and act on
    information
- Evidence type:
  analytical chapter
- Best chapters:
  2, 10, 12
- Manuscript-safe use:
  use to complicate simplistic claims that tacit knowledge is either fully
  uncapturable or fully captured
- Status:
  use with care
